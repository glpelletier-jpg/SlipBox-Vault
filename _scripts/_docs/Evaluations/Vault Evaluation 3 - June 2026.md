## Major discovery: literature notes have two formats

The 38 literature notes split into two distinct types:

**Type A — 7 Readwise imports (reformatted):** These had sparse highlights from Kindle. They now have 3 synthesized key ideas, a personal reaction, and connection to active quests and work context.

**Type B — 31 detailed chapter summaries:** These were imported from a previous PKM system and contain rich, chapter-by-chapter content — bullet points, direct quotes, frameworks, diagrams references. *These are not empty.* They don't match the template format, but they contain substantially more raw content than the Type A notes.

**What this means:** The Type B notes are actually the most valuable material for generating permanent notes. Feel Good Productivity, for example, has complete chapter summaries covering play, power, people, unblocking, and sustaining — more than enough content to write 5-10 permanent notes. The work to do is mining them, not reformatting them.

The vault now has 38 literature notes full of synthesized content and 3 MOCs identifying dozens of ready-to-write claims. The prerequisites have never been better. The bottleneck is writing the first handful of permanent notes that give the slip box something to link to.
