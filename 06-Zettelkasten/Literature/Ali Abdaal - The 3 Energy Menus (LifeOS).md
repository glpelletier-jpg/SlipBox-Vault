# Ali Abdaal — The 3 Energy Menus (LifeOS)

## Core Claim

> When motivation, focus, or energy falters, having a pre-built menu of personal tactics eliminates the decision cost of recovery — letting you act immediately rather than deliberate.

---

## The 3 Menus

### 🔥 Activation Menu
**Trigger:** Can't start anything. Pre-task dread, poor sleep, decision paralysis.
**Function:** Generates just enough momentum to begin.
**Examples from source:** gentle background music, short breathing exercise, small admin task.

### 🔄 Reactivation Menu
**Trigger:** Mind wanders mid-task. Daydreaming. Focus has already slipped.
**Function:** Snaps attention back to the current task without a full context switch.
**Examples from source:** one-minute stretch, energy boost snack, jumping jacks, upbeat playlist swap, phone moved to another room.

### 🔋 Recharge Menu
**Trigger:** Energy depleted after sustained output. Pre-burnout state.
**Function:** Replenishes cognitive and physical resources before continuing.
**Examples from source:** short walk, coffee/tea, shoulder rolls, desk stretch.

---

## Key Insight

The menus are **personal and pre-decided**. The value is not the tactics themselves but the elimination of friction — when depleted, you execute a known routine rather than burning more cognitive resources choosing what to do.

---

## Synthesis Notes

- Pairs well with Pomodoro / time-blocking: Recharge Menu fires at end of session, Reactivation fires when a Pomodoro is interrupted.
- Could be embedded directly in the Daily Note as a collapsible callout or linked from the morning check-in section.
- The Activation Menu addresses **initiation resistance** — the most common failure mode in deep work.
