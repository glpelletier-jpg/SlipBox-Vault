## Key ideas (in your own words)

1. Every electrical circuit is built on three fundamental quantities: voltage (electrical pressure), current (flow of electrons), and resistance (opposition to flow). Ohm's Law ties them together: V = IR. Every calculation in circuit analysis is a variation on this relationship.

2. Atoms have electrons in their outer shells that can be pushed from atom to atom — this flow of electrons is electric current. Understanding current at this level explains why insulators, conductors, and semiconductors behave differently in industrial instrumentation.

3. Circuit analysis is the foundation for everything in electronics — understanding how a PLC reads a sensor signal, how a VFD drives a motor, how a 4-20mA loop works. It's not abstract; it's the physics behind every piece of industrial hardware.

## Quotes worth keeping

> Circuit analysis is one of basic subjects in engineering and particularly important for Electrical and Electronics students.

> Everything in the universe is made of atoms and every atom consists of 3 types of particles, neutrons, protons and electrons. Neutrons and protons are packed together in the nucleus and make up the center of an atom, whereas the electrons move around the nucleus in a constant motion.

> The SI unit of electric charge is Coulomb (C) and it is the charge possessed by 6.24 x 1018 electrons.
