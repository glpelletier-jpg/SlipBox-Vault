## Chapter 01 - PLAY

#### Create an Adventure

- Kids play by enjoying activities without worrying about results

- As we age (get older) the spirit of adventure gets squeezed out of us

- Adventure is the first major ingredient of play and happiness

- An adventurous life holds the key to unlocking positive emotions

**CHOOSE YOUR CHARACTER**

- When you take on a different persona, you start to find adventure

**The 8 Play Personalities**

1. Collector

2. Competitor

3. Explorer

4. Creator

5. Storyteller

6. Joker

7. Director

8. Kinesthete

Reflect on which of these characters you identify the most, try to approach your work as if you are that character. I'm 1,2,3 mostly

**EMBRACE YOU CURIOUSITY**

- When you're curious about something you remember the details better

- Engaging with curiosity makes you feel good and you become better at retaining information

- Curiosity allows you to focus longer

- Think of your life as containing a series of side quests

- Add a side quest to your day, to create space for curiosity, exploration and playfulness

- Discover something amazing and unexpected on the way

> [!quote] "In every job that must be done, there is an element of fun. You find the fun and snap. The job is gone" - Mary Poppins

- What would this look like if it was fun?

- Think of a task you don't want to do and ask yourself what it would look like if it was fun.

**ENJOY THE PROCESS, NOT THE OUTCOME**

- By learning to focus on the process, rather than the outcome, you're more likely to enjoy the task.

- For play to flourish, we need to create an environment that's low stakes and fosters relaxation.

**REFRAME YOUR FAILURE**

- Humans are hugely disproportionately impacted by negative consequences. These make us afraid of failure, even when we don't need to be.

- Success is about how you frame your failures

- No failure is just a failure. It's an invitation to try something new

**DON'T BE SERIOUS, BE SINCERE**

- When you feel like your work is draining or overwhelming, try ask yourself "How can I approach this with a little less seriousness and a little more sincerity"

### Play Chapter Summarised

- Seriousness is overrated. Approach work with as sense of play otherwise being serious could ruin your life

- Approach life tasks with a sense of adventure. See life as a game filled with surprises and side quests.

- Find the Fun, there is an element of fun in every task. Ask yourself, what would this be like if it were fun?

- Lower the stakes, failures are only failures when you think they are. Approach work with less seriousness and more sincerity.

## Chapter 02 - POWER

- Most common explanation for all business success is culture

- Start thinking about power differently

- Personal empowerment - your job is in your control, your life is in your hands, decisions about your future are yours alone

- Power isn't something to exert on others

**BOOST YOUR CONFIDENCE**

- Feeling good and feeling confident about your ability to complete a task masks us feel good when we're doing it and helps us do it better

- Believing you can is the first step to making sure you actually can

**THE CONFIDENCE SWITCH**

- Confidence is something you learn

- When you're not confident, ask yourself what would it look like if I approached this task feeling confident.

**THE SOCIAL MODEL METHOD**

- Other peoples examples boost our confidence

- This demonstrates challenges that can be overcome

- [Indie Hackers Podcasts](https://www.indiehackers.com/podcasts)

- Find people going through the same challenges as you and spend more time with them

- Learning through doing is one of the most powerful forces in human psychology.

- The more you do something, the greater your sense of control

**THE SHOSHIN APPROACH**

- Shoshin refers to a state of mind in which we approach every task and situation with the curiosity, openness and humility of a beginner. See [Shoshin: This Zen Concept Will Help You Stop Being a Slave to Old Beliefs](https://jamesclear.com/shoshin)

- A beginner is more willing to try things out even if they might fail

- Our experiences make us set in our ways

- Shoshin shows us to see things afresh

- Beginners are willing to make mistakes which is what's needed to learn

**THE PROTEGE EFFECT**

- People who teach a subject, learn the material better

- He who teaches learns

- You don't need to be a guru, just be a guide

**TAKE OWNERSHIP OF YOUR WORK**

- Edward Deci and Richard Ryan - 1981 Self Determination Theory

- Motivation falls on a spectrum of extrinsic vs intrinsic values.

- Intrinsic is more powerful that extrinsic values

**THE MOTIVATION SPECTRUM**

**Extrinsic Motivation**

- Reward and Punishment

- Social Approval

- Performance Goals

**Intrinsic Motivation**

- Self Fulfilment

- Curiosity and Learning

- Personal Growth

**OWN THE PROCESS**

- When we can't take ownership of the situation we can still take ownership of the process.

**OWN YOUR MINDSET**

- "I get to do this" instead of "Why do I have to do this"

- Switching from "have to" to "choose to" boosts your sense of control, power and what you're capable of.

### Power Chapter Summary

- Power is a scary word, but it doesn't have to be

- 3 ways to increase your power, confidence, level up your skills and take ownership of your mindset.

## Chapter 03 - PEOPLE

**Competitor Mindset**

- You win, I lose

- My Success

- I rise by outdoing others

**Comrade Mindset**

- You win, I win

- Our Success

- We rise by lifting others

- When we work in synchrony with other people, we tend to be more productive

- When we work to help others, our brain releases a flood of chemicals which create a natural high

- Random acts of kindness

- Ask for help from others, cause it makes them feel better

- Empathise positives when asking for help

- Avoid negative phrases like "I feel really bad for asking", "If you help me, I will do xyz"

- Lying is associated with the activation of the limbic system, the same area of the brain that initiates the fight of flight response.

### People Chapter Summary

- Life is more fun with friends around. There are some people who naturally uplift our Energy - the trick is finding them.

- Treat people you work with as comrades rather than competitors

- We rarely help others or ask for help

- "What can I do to brighten someone's day?"

## Chapter 04 - Seek Clarity

### Seek Clarity

- If only you wanted to succeed as badly as you wanted to breathe it would happen

- Discipline is when we do stuff that we don't feel like doing

- Motivation and Discipline are useful strategies to beat procrastination but they cover up deeper wounds. The unblock method encourages us to understand why we feel bad about work in the first place.

[image: Unblock Seek Clarity 2024-02-10 14.40.17.excalidraw]

### THE FOG OF UNCERTAINTY

- Overwhelmed by the unknowns or the complexity of a situation leading to an inability to act

- We don't start because we don't know what we're supposed to be doing in the first place

- Uncertainty makes us feel bad, and achieve less. Humans prefer predictability and stability

**ASK WHY**

- If we don't ask why we're embarking on any given project, it's near impossible to get on with actually doing it

- Auftragstaktik - Mission Type Tactics

1. Purpose behind the operation

2. End State that the commander is aiming for

3. Key Tasks that the commander felt should be taken to accomplish the objective.

**Experiment - Using commanders intent Auftragstaktik**

1. What is the purpose behind this?

2. Build a To-Do-List from there

**Experiment - The Five Whys**

- You need to remind yourself of the big "why" every day, every hour

- Asking why repeatedly reminds us of what we should really be focussing on

**ASK "WHAT"**

- Moving from "Why" to "What"

**Experiment - Nice Goals**

- Turning purpose into a plan involves some goal setting

- If we obsess over a goal, we lose track of the intrinsic pleasure that may have come from the task.

[image: NICE Goals 2024-02-15 10.16.44.excalidraw]

### Use SMART Goals vs NICE Goals

**Smart Goal**

- Get a promotion to a senior management position within two years **Nice Goal**

- Dedicate an hour each week to improving one key skill or networking with industry professionals

- With NICE Goals in sight, you should have a clearer sense of what you need to do

**ASK WHEN**

- If you don't know when you're doing something, chances are you won't do it

- Implementation intentions - best formula is a conditional statement. If X happens then I will Y

- IF Then prompts alter peoples long term behaviour

- When you no longer need to think about when you'll do it. You'll just do it

**TIME BLOCKING**

- Put aside time in your calendar (time block)

### SEEK CLARITY Chapter Summary

- When we feel bad, we achieve less. What's blocking your good mood?

- The first emotion barrier is uncertainty. The solution is to gain clarity by asking why and figuring out how

- NICE Goals are better than SMART Goals

- Ask When - IF Then Statements

## Chapter 05 - Find Courage

- Lack of talent or inspiration doesn't hold you back. It's fear

- Fear is a negative emotion that blocks our productivity

**KNOW YOUR FEAR**

- Getting to know your fear is the first step towards overcoming it

- When you're procrastinating ask yourself, what am I afraid of? This is known as affective labelling

- Put your feelings into words, which forces you to identify and get to know the sensations you're experiencing

- Where does this fear come from?

- Labels society place on us profoundly affect the way we behave. The labels we give ourselves affect our behaviour.

- A positive label can overcome negative labels

- Self-Doubt = Life long Learner

- A lifelong learner is constantly looking for new ways to improve themself.

- If we change our labels, we can often change our behaviour

**REDUCE YOUR FEAR**

- When we're scared, we become paralysed

- The 10/10/10 Rule

- Reduce Fear by gaining perspective

- Cognitive reappraisal is the practice of changing the interpretation of a situation so that we feel emotionally better.

- Remind yourself, the thing you're feeling so bad about probably won't matter in the future

[image: The101010Rule 2024-02-16 18.52.44.excalidraw]

- How confident do I actually need to feel to just get started with this?

- One of the most common forces that drives our procrastination is the fear of being seen

**STOP SPOTLIGHTING**

- The truth is, everyone is concerned mostly about themselves, and how they're coming across

- No one cares....

- The mindset of "no one cares" can be totally transformative. Reduces anxiety related procrastination.

- The Batman Effect allows us to overcome fear or procrastination by visualising ourself in an alter ego

- Mantras like "I'm confident, I'm fearless, I'm unstoppable"

### Find Courage Chapter 5 Summary

- Our Emotional blocker is fear. The solution is to get the courage to face it

- That courage comes from 3 sources.

1. Understand your fear

2. Ask yourself: why have I not started on that task or project

3. What am I afraid of? Where does this fear come from?

- Reduce your fear to stop yourself catastrophising by using the 10/10/10 method.

- If you're scared of what people think, remind yourself that most people are not, in fact, thinking about you.

- We're self conscious species, but we're not usually a judgemental one.

## Chapter 6 - Get Started

- Newtons first law of motion "An object at rest stays at rest, while an object in motion stays in motion, unless acted on by an external imbalanced force."

**REDUCE FRICTION**

- Tweak your environment to make things you want

- Have a trigger object for the habit

- CBA = Can't be arsed

- The most common and most paralysing obstacle to just getting started is CBA

- Tackle CBA using the 5 minute rule

- Commit to working on a task for 5 mins

- You don't always have to continue after 5 minutes

When you procrastinate ask yourself

- What's the next step

Example: When you don't want to do Yoga, roll out your mat and stand on it

Long Term Goal

- Write a book translates into write a page or a few words

**TRACK YOUR PROGRESS**

- For a writer, track your word count

- For a walker, track your steps

- Tracking progress provides you with tangible evidence that you're moving towards your goal

**Learning a new skill**

- Keep a learning journal

- Write down aha moments

- Record what you have learnt

- Your objective is to find ways to encourage yourself as you work towards your goals. Hold yourself accountable as you go.

**Find an Accountability buddy**

- When we find a partner to hold us accountable we're much more likely to overcome inertia

- Five Criteria = Disciplined, Challenging, Patient, Supportive and Constructive

**FORGIVE YOURSELF**

- Beating yourself up is a bigger issue than procrastination

- Find the WIN, I didn't do X but I did do Y

- You can focus on small losses or celebrate small wins

## Summary

- The most common blocker is emotional. Inertia, when you're doing nothing, it's easy to carry on doing nothing. When you're working it's much easier to carry on working.

- Best antidote to doing nothing is simple to doing something. Define your next step and track your progress so you have evidence you're moving towards your goal.

- Celebrate the small wins, be kind to yourself

### Part 3 - SUSTAIN

#### BURNOUT

1. Overexertion Burnout

- Packing too much into each day

- These come from the negative emotions that arise when we do too much, too fast. In other words we sprint all the time

**DO LESS SO THAT YOU CAN UNLOCK MORE**

[image: Overexertion 2024-02-24 16.42.38.excalidraw]

- We tend to think we can do everything. This is a myth

- If it's not hell yeah, it's not worth doing

- Highest performers switch between tasks but don't go overboard. How by adding friction

- Think of distractions as a temporary veering off track, not an indication that it's time to abandon plans altogether.

- Give yourself permission to be distracted

- Adding a break of ten minutes, between two tasks that require self control seems to help overexertion

- 52 minutes of work, 17 minutes of rest (do nothing)

### Schedule your breaks

- Schedule time in your calendar to do nothing

- Knowledge work requires self-regulatory exertions

- The ability to control our behaviour, thoughts and feelings

- We need to find ways to replenish our energy

- Break aren't a special treat, they're absolutely necessity

- Unplanned rests are energising distractions

- Life isn't about maintaining focus all the time. It's about allowing space for little moments of serenaded and joy

- Using energy in the wrong way = misalignment burnout

- Our mood suffers due to no deep periods of time off which leads to depletion burnout

- Mood suffers when you put all your efforts into something that doesn't bring joy or meaning which is known as depletion burnout.

### Summary

- The greatest cause of burnout is low mood. If you make yourself feel better, you'll last longer.

- Overexertion can be solved by doing less

- Stop yourself from overcommitting. Get comfortable saying no. Put your energy into one project

- Resist Distraction

- Make breaks a special event and take more of them

### Recharge Summary

- Depletion Burnout results from not giving yourself enough time or space to truly recharge

- CALM = Find an activity or project that makes you feel competent, autonomous, liberated and mellow.

## Chapter 9 - ALIGN

- Not all extrinsic motivation is inherently bad

- When we think about death, we get a clearer view of life

#### Types of Motivation

**External** = I'm doing this because important people will like and respect me more if I do

**Introjected** = I'm doing this because I'll feel guilty or bad about myself if I don't

**Identified** = I'm doing this because I truly value the goal it's helping me work towards.

**Intrinsic** = I'm doing this because I love the process as an end in itself

[image: Types-of-Motivation 2024-02-25 13.20.50.excalidraw]

#### Eulogy Method

- Focus on your funeral. What would I feel good about someone saying in my eulogy.

- This helps ask what do I value from other peoples perspectives

#### The Odyssey Plan "Design your Life"

1. **What is your current path?**

- What your life looks like if you continue down your current path

1. **Your alternative path?**

- What your life would look like in 5 years if you took a completely different path

1. **Your radical path?**

- What your life would look like in 5 years from now if you took a completely different path where money, social obligations, what people think is irrelevant.

- Misalignment burnouts arise when we spend time on goals that don't match up with our sense of self. Overcome these by changing your behaviour.

- Reflect on accomplishments you'd want to celebrate in a years time.

- What are the three actions today that might take you a little step closer to the life you want in a year.

- The only way to escape procrastination and burnout is to find joy in your situation.

- Adopt an experimental outlook

- Figure out what works, discard the rest

- Enjoy the process, it's not about perfection
