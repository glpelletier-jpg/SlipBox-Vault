# Quarterly Quests

## 🚀 Quarterly Quests

Align your daily actions with your long-term goals through Quarterly Quests. In the areas of work and life, we will choose one single most important priority project to work on for the next 90 days, a short enough timeframe to make meaningful progress that moves us closer to our envisioned future.

### 🥜 In a Nutshell

- Every 90 days, choose your priority projects as "Quests"

- For both WORK and LIFE domains, define:

    - **1 Main Quest:** A specific, measurable, meaningful goal that aligns with your long-term vision

    - **Up to 3 Side Quests**: These complement your main quests but have narrower scope. Also optional if not completed

- **Review your Quests:**

    - Daily in your Morning Manifesto

    - Weekly during the Weekly Review

    - Reset your Quests every 90 days

- Result: Clear priorities, better focus on needle-moving activities, and balanced progress in work and life

## 🏴‍☠️ Quarterly Quests: Where Vision Meets Action

**Quarterly Quests is where the rubber meets the road** in your LifeOS journey. It serves as the crucial bridge between the visionary thinking of your Life Compass and Future Sketch, and the daily execution enabled by your Focused Hour Formula [https://www.notion.so/Focused-Hour-Formula-1721a88ee85c80958678ca34070eb25a?pvs=21](https://www.notion.so/Focused-Hour-Formula-1721a88ee85c80958678ca34070eb25a?pvs=21)and Productive Day Protocol.

**Think of Quarterly Quests as your strategic sweet spot** - not so far out that it feels abstract (like a 3-year vision), but not so immediate that you get lost in the day-to-day (like your daily to-do list). The 90-day timeframe is long enough to accomplish something meaningful, yet short enough to maintain focus and motivation. This is where your long-term aspirations, as defined by your Life Compass and Future Sketch, begin to take concrete shape.

We call these 90-day goals **"Quests"** for a reason. Like the quests of ancient tales, each one represents a meaningful journey with a clear objective. By designating certain projects as Quests, you elevate them above the noise of daily life and give them the focus they deserve.

**The system is deliberately structured to maintain balance while driving progress:**

- 1 *Main Quest* each for *Work* and *Life* ensures you're moving forward in both domains

- Up to 3 *Side Quests* per domain provide space for supporting initiatives without losing focus

**When you spread your energy across many projects, you don't actually make 10% progress** on 10 different things. In reality, you might make only 5% progress on each due to the mental, physical, and emotional overhead of context-switching. Each time you change tasks or projects, there's friction and cognitive cost.

[https://geni.us/r9IqHMr](https://geni.us/r9IqHMr)Greg McKeownillustrates this through his "less but better" philosophy. By focusing intensely on one main priority (your Main Quest) with just a few supporting priorities (Side Quests), you can make dramatic progress in what truly matters rather than inch forward on many fronts. As Cal Newport emphasises in his work, this isn't about doing less for the sake of doing less, but about recognising that our attention and productivity follow a law of diminishing returns when spread too thin.

### ⛵︎ The Different Quests

Along our journey to our compelling 3-year future sketch, we will tackle our goals in 90-day check points. To advance in both *Work* and *Life* levels, we will establish the following quests:

- **Main Quest:** Identify the single most important goal for your work and one for your personal life. Ask yourself, “What is the one thing that would make the biggest difference in the next 90 days?”

- **Side Quests:** If you’d like, choose 2-3 supporting goals that complement your Main Quest but aren’t as critical.



# Areas, Projects & Tasks

## 📋 Areas, Projects & Tasks - The Missing Link in Traditional Goal-Setting

Many goal-setting systems fall into one of two traps: they're either too abstract to act on, or too tactical to maintain direction. Quarterly Quests solves this by creating a structured hierarchy:

### **Vision → Quarterly Quests → Areas → Projects → Tasks**

This hierarchy ensures that your daily actions (Tasks) are organized into meaningful groups (Projects), (Area), with the most important initiatives elevated to Quest status, all aligned with your bigger picture (Vision).

- Projects can quickly multiply - before you know it, you have 20+ active projects

- All projects start feeling equally important

- Easy to spread yourself too thin trying to progress on everything

- Hard to know which projects deserve your best time and energy

- Can lead to lots of projects moving forward slowly rather than meaningful completion

- The Solution → Choosing Priority Projects as "Quests"

### 🧰 Areas: Collections of Projects

Areas are categories or spheres of life where your projects live. Common way to organise areas, which we recommend to avoid overthinking and overhead:

- Work: Everything professional, like sales, Marketing, Operations, Admin

- Life: Everything personal, like Health, Family, Home, Relationships, Personal Growth

Why this works:

- Reduces decision fatigue

- Prevents over-categorisation

- Forces clear work/life boundaries

### 🧱 Projects: Collections of Related Tasks

### A project is a collection of related tasks working towards a specific outcome. Projects:

- Have a clear deliverable or end state

- Take multiple work sessions to complete

- Should be achievable within 90 days

- Are tracked in your weekly review

**Examples of Projects**

- Website redesign

- Q3 marketing campaign

- Home office setup

- Family summer vacation planning

### ✅ Tasks: The Building Blocks

A task is the smallest unit of work - something you can complete in one sitting. Tasks should:

- Start with a verb (draft, create, review, etc.)

- Be completable in one work session

- Have a clear end state

Tasks are also often stored in a “Task Manager” or “Todo List” like a paper list, Todoist, Things3, Apple Reminders, and Google Keep.

The trouble with just thinking about tasks is that tasks in isolation can feel overwhelming - like an endless list of "stuff to do". It’s hard to see how individual tasks connect to bigger goals. It’s also easy to lose sight of the bigger picture. If we just focus on tasks, our work days can lead to completing lots of tasks but not making meaningful progress. It’s also quite difficult to prioritise when everything looks like an equal-sized task. The solution is to organise tasks by project.

**Examples of Tasks**

- Draft email to marketing team about new campaign

- Schedule dentist appointment

- Create wireframe for homepage

- Review and comment on Sarah's proposal

**📁 Quick Note:** What's a task for one person might be a project for another. If you're new to something, break it down into smaller tasks. As you get more experienced, entire projects might become single tasks.

# The GPS Method

## 🌐 The GPS Method

The GPS method dramatically boosts your chances of completing your Quests. Use it primarily for Main Quests, and optionally for Side Quests.



## Stage 1: Define Your Goal

Define your “Main Questˮ and use these prompts to highlight the purpose.

🎯 **What:** What goal are we working towards? What’s our desired outcome?

🥰 **Why:** Why do we care about this? Why is it important to us?

### **Example: Starting a YouTube channel**

- **What is the goal?** To start my YouTube channel and post my first 7 videos

- **Why does it matter to you?** Because starting a channel has been a dream of mine for years and I want to finally prove to myself that I can do it.

### **Example: Applying for a research grant**

- **What is the goal?** To apply for the MacMillan research grant to fund my PhD Dissertation fieldwork.

- **Why does it matter to you?** Applying for and potentially receiving a research grant will allow me to travel and conduct my study which will move my dissertation forward, meaning I am one step closer to the ultimate goal of graduating.

## Stage 2: Make a Plan

Break your goal into actionable steps and schedule time blocks for each.

🗒️ **Tasks:** What are the specific actions that’ll get us there?

🕰️ **Time:** When are we going to work on the thing? Use recurring calendar events, etc, to find time dedicated to the quest.

### **Example: Run my first half marathon**

- **Tasks:** Progressive training schedule building to race day

- **Time:** I will schedule training days for Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Saturday with rest days on rest days on Wednesday, Friday, and Sunday

### **Example: Launch my website editing business**

- **Tasks**:

    - Reach out to friends and mutuals looking for website assistance

    - Create a landing page & newsletter

    - Advertise services on social media 4 days per week

- **Time**: I will dedicate a 1 hour block per day to create content to promote the business and a 3-hour block every Wednesday to work on client sites or build portfolio websites for promotional material

## Stage 3: Create a System

Create systems to help you stick to the plan:

🪵 **Reminders (to stay on track):** How will we strategically remind ourselves to continue working towards this goal?

- Daily reminder - Morning Manifesto

- Weekly reminder - Weekly Review

- Calendar notifications

📶 **Tracking (to measure progress)**: How will we track our progress towards the goal?

- Progress metrics (colour-coding, progress bars, check boxes)

- Celebrate milestones

- Weekly check-ins

🤝 **Accountability**: Who can help us when our motivation (inevitably) dips?

- Friendly reminders from your partner or friends

- Accountability groups (e.g. Productivity Lab)

🔥**Hot tip**: When creating your plan, ask "If I follow this plan perfectly, what are my chances of achieving the goal?" Keep refining until you're at least 90% confident.

### **🥅 The 90-Day Rule**

All Quests and Projects should be completable within 90 days. If something will take longer:

1. Break it down into 90-day chunks

2. Focus on what you can actually accomplish in one quarter

3. Make that your Quest

Example: Instead of "Write a book" (too big), make your Quest "Complete first draft of chapters 1-3" or "Submit book proposal".

### 🔍 Periodic Quest Reviews

To keep your Quests on track, check in regularly to monitor progress and remind yourself of these quests. The power of Quests comes from regular attention and strategic reminders. Even if you forget everything else, just asking "What's my Main Quest and how's it going?" each morning will keep you moving towards what matters most.

We recommend checking in with your quests in the following periods:

- Daily: Check Main Quests in Morning Manifesto

- Weekly: Review all Quests in Weekly Review

- Quarterly: Reset Quests every 90 days

### ⁉️ Frequently Asked Questions

❓**Writing down goals and plans is easy - actually doing them is hard. How does GPS actually help with execution?**

Execution is where most systems fall apart. That's exactly why the 'S' in GPS (System) is crucial. By building in regular check-ins, specific time blocks, and accountability partners, we're creating a support structure that kicks in exactly when motivation dips. It's like having training wheels until the new behaviours become natural.

❓**What happens when external factors completely derail your plan? Isn't this too rigid?**

This is where the weekly reviews come in clutch. They're your chance to adapt and adjust as life happens. Think of your GPS as a navigation system. When there's a roadblock, you don't abandon the destination, you just recalculate the route.

❓**Daily, weekly,** ***and*** **quarterly reviews? That sounds like a lot of reminders.**

We need reminders to make sure we don’t forget our most needle-moving quest for the quarter. The daily check-in is literally just glancing at your Main Quest while writing your Morning Manifesto (takes 30 seconds). The weekly review is part of your regular planning, and quarterly is just four times a year. It's like checking your map occasionally to make sure you're still heading in the right direction.

❓**Won't frequent reviews make you second-guess your goals and keep changing direction?**

usually, the opposite tends to happen. Regular reviews help you stay committed to your main goals while adjusting your tactics. Think of it like sailing - you're constantly making small adjustments to stay on course, not changing your destination. The Main Quest stays fixed while your approach can evolve based on what you learn.
