> [!info]  The true power of Obsidian is being able to link your notes together. By understanding how information relates to one another, you can improve your memory and recall. This process also helps you form deeper understandings of ideas and concepts throughout your Vault.    Obsidian uses wiki-style links, which look like this: `[](Link%20to%20your%20Note%20Name)`

## Creating your first Link

To create a new link, enclose your text in double left square brackets

`[](Your%20First%20Link)`

You can also create links to notes that doesn't exist yet following the same method and then CTRL + Click to Create the New Note based off that Link.

Obsidian Autocompletes existing notes in your Vault so when you type `[[` a helpful window will appear.

## How to Navigate between notes?

[image: linking-your-notes-01.png]

> [!tip] If you're a more Visual thinker, you can use the Local Graph View or Graph View.

> [!example] How to Access the Local Graph View

1. The Local Graph can be accessed upper-right corner of the note. Click **More options** (three dots).

2. Select **Open linked view**.

3. Select **Open local graph**. Click any of the nodes in the graph to navigate to that note.

4. You can navigate between your note with Backlinks Tab on the Right Sidebar This will show Linked mentions

> [!example] How to Access the Graph View Hotkey CTRL+G opens the Graph View which we will cover in Exploring the local graph and graph view in Section 3: Unlocking Obsidian's Power
