# Balanced Weeks



# The Balanced Week Blueprint

## ⚖️Introduction to Balanced Weeks

**Working harder won’t lead you to success**—working sustainably alongside living a life you love will. Balanced Weeks is your blueprint for making that happen, showing you how to build a weekly rhythm that aligns with your goals while preserving time for what matters most. The following guide will show you how to create structure without sacrificing flexibility.

## 🥜 In a Nutshell

- A balanced week combines work, rest, and personal priorities

- **Weekly Review**: Each week, take 30 minutes to look back and plan ahead:

    - **Reflect**: Review how the past week went and celebrate wins

    - **Plan**: Define top 3 priorities for the week ahead

- **Ideal Week**: Create and maintain an ideal week that reflects priorities

- Result: Weeks that feel aligned with your ideal envisioned life

## ☘️ The Balanced Week Blueprint

Ideally, a balanced week combines work, rest, and personal priorities, including health, relationships, and hobbies. Crucially, you can have this balance even while having a full-time job.

A balanced week consists of time split between:

- **Work**: Deep focus sessions, meetings, admin tasks

- **Life**: Rest, hobbies, relationships, health, personal growth



# The Weekly Review

## 🔦 The Weekly Review

**The Weekly Review is like a personal strategy session done once a week.**

**It's a 15-30 minute check-in** to reflect on the past week and plan for the one ahead. I first heard of the Weekly Review from [[Getting Things Done]] by David Allen. It's a really good book. He has an elaborate process for the weekly review, but I found his process too overwhelming.

This is my recommended structure:

- **Remind**: Recall your priorities, goals, and quests

- **Reflect**: Look back at the week and celebrate your wins

- **Plan**: Define your priorities and insert them into your calendar

The Weekly Review is a keystone habit that can transform your productivity, even if you only spend 5 minutes on it. It's your weekly touchpoint to remind yourself of priorities, reflect on progress, and plan for what's ahead. Don't let perfectionism stop you. A quick 5-minute check-in is infinitely better than no review at all.

Think of your Weekly Review as a living, breathing practice that evolves with you. While you can start with the template questions I use, feel free to adapt them to your needs over time.

The reason why a lot of people don’t stick to their New Year’s resolutions is because they don’t remind themselves of what they were. A Weekly Review is a good reminder to keep strategically orienting ourselves towards our intended goals.

### **Example: Ali’s Weekly Review**

- **Remind**

    - Quarterly Quests: What were our quarterly quests? Howʼre they going?

    - Weekly Priorities: What were last weekʼs 3 priorities? How did they go?

- **Reflect**

    - Celebrations: What were the good and great things that happened last week?

    - Challenges: What were last weekʼs challenges? In what ways did we not act in line with our intentions for the week? And what would we like to change (if anything) next week?

- **Plan**

    - Predict: Look ahead to the next 2 weeks. Does anything need scheduling in the calendar, or any tasks need adding to our todo list?

    - Weekly Priorities: What are our top 3 outcomes for the upcoming week. Letʼs make sure those are in the calendar.

    - Organise: Have we updated our Tasks and Projects List?
