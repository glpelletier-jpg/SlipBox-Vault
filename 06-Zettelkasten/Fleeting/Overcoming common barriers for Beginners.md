> [!info]

1. ***Steep learning curve:*** Obsidian has many powerful features, but new users may find it overwhelming to learn and navigate.

2. ***Time investment:*** Building a comprehensive knowledge base takes time, which can be a deterrent for busy individuals.

3. ***Technical know-how:*** While Obsidian doesn’t require coding skills, users still need to be comfortable with technology and understand concepts like Markdown.

4. ***Lack of structure:*** The open-ended nature of Obsidian can make it challenging for some users to establish an effective organization system.

---

### 1. Steep Learning Curve

- **Start Simple:** Focus on the core functionalities first - creating notes, linking them, and basic formatting. Explore more advanced features as you get comfortable.

- **Utilise Resources:** There are tons of resources available! Look for beginner guides, video tutorials, and community forums specifically geared towards new Obsidian users.

- **Focus on the "Why":** Remind yourself of the benefits of using Obsidian - improved organization, better knowledge retention, and a central hub for your ideas. This motivation can help you persevere through the initial learning curve.

[image: Overcome-Steep-Learning-Curve.png]

---

### 2. Time Investment

- **Start Small:** Don't try to build a comprehensive system overnight. Begin by incorporating Obsidian into your existing workflow, for example, by taking notes during meetings or jotting down ideas throughout the day.

- **Focus on Consistency:** Aim for small, consistent efforts. Daily or weekly note-taking sessions, even for short periods, will gradually build your knowledge base.

- **Leverage Templates:** Utilise pre-made templates for common note types like meeting notes, project outlines, or daily journals. This saves time and provides a starting point for organization.

[image: Overcome-Time-Investment.png]

---

### Technical Know-How

- **Learn as You Go:** Don't be afraid to experiment! Obsidian has a gentle learning curve for Markdown syntax. Start with basic formatting like bold and italics, and gradually explore more as needed.

- **Community Support:** Don't hesitate to ask for help! The Obsidian community is incredibly active and supportive. Utilize forums, Discord channels, or Reddit to ask questions and learn from others.

- **Focus on the Basics:** You don't need to master every technical feature to benefit from Obsidian. Start with the core functionalities that align with your needs.

[image: Overcome-Technical-Know-How.png]

---

### Lack of Structure

- **Start with Templates:** As mentioned earlier, pre-made templates provide a solid foundation for organization. You can customize them further as your system evolves.

- **Explore Community Vaults:** Many Obsidian users share their pre-configured vaults with community resources. Explore these to get inspiration and see how others structure their knowledge base.

- **Find Your System:** The beauty of Obsidian is its flexibility. Experiment with different organizational methods - tags, folders, backlinks - and find the approach that works best for you.

[image: Overcome-Lack-of-Structure.png]
