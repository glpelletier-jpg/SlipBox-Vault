# Productive Days

## Introduction to the Productive Day Protocol

Ever feel like your days are running you, instead of you running your days? Shift from reactive to proactive through planning for the day, focusing, and an evening shutdown routine to make meaningful progress towards your ideal life.



## 🥜 In a Nutshell

The Productive Day Protocol has three components:

- **The Morning Manifesto**: A 5-minute check in to begin the day

- **Focus Hours**: 3-5 blocks of deep work

- **Evening Shutdown**: Shut down “work mode” and boot up “life mode”

👉 Result: Doing deep work that brings you closer to wards your envisioned goals



## 🧠 The Productive Day Protocol

Plan and prepare for your ideal, realistic, and most productive days.

**A Productive Day is about moving from reactive to proactive**. Reactive is when you're reacting to all the stuff coming your way: being pinged on Slack, getting emails, being given tasks, and dealing with admin piling up. You're simply reacting to what's coming your way.

While some jobs are naturally reactive (like air traffic control), most of us could be more proactive in our days to make meaningful progress towards our most important priorities.

A Productive Day starts with the Morning Manifesto to set our intentions and prime ourselves for the work session. Then we block off about four hours in the day for deep, focused work. At the end of the work day, we close off with an Evening Shutdown to set a clear boundary between work and life.

# The Morning Manifesto

## ☀️ The Morning Manifesto

> It is wonderful how much work can be got through in a day, if we go by the rule—map out our time, divide it off, and take up one thing regularly after another. To drift through our work, or to rush through it in a helter-skelter fashion, ends in comparatively little being done. “One thing at a timeˮ will always perform a better dayʼs work than doing two or three things at a time. By following this rule, one person will do more in a day than another does in a week. - Thomas Mitchell, Essays on Life

**The Morning Manifesto is a 5-minute checklist** that sets you up for a productive, joyful day. It's the daily keystone habit to which you can attach any other components of your LifeOS. It's a living document that you update as your context changes.

This checklist approach was inspired by [The Checklist Manifesto](https://geni.us/qmY1cQ) by Dr. Atul Gawande, a book that transformed surgical safety in hospitals. The book shows that humans are prone to errors when relying only on intuition, skills, or habits. By using a checklist to follow the same steps every time, we can make complex tasks simpler and more reliable.



☀️ We recommend having three sections for the Morning Manifesto:

- **Prime**: The idea behind this is to prime ourselves to get ready for the day with a little bit of reflection and gratitude.

- **Align**: I check in with my Quarterly Quests every day to remind myself of them. I also remind myself of our top three outcomes for the week and what today’s adventure going to be. In case I forget to do my weekly review, the Morning Manifesto gives me a chance to define my top three outcomes for the week.

- **Organise**: Here I track and update my projects and to-do lists, and stick tasks into my calendar.

**‼️ Warning: Keep the Morning Manifesto to five minutes** ‼️

⚠️ **When you first start this practice, there's a huge temptation to write loads** and loads of questions because you're feeling motivated. You might unintentionally create this enormous list:

- I'm going to do half an hour of journaling every day

- I’m going to ask myself what I'm grateful for, why I love my kids, what my plan is to quit my job

- I’m going track my step count, check my sleep score, monitor my calories

**Please don't do this.** I've tried it. It doesn't work.



When building a habit, **we want to start small**.

It's much more important to get your Morning Manifesto done in less than five minutes so you actually do it. If it takes any longer, it just won’t happen when life gets busy because doing it is too much friction. This is especially true when life feels overwhelming, which is precisely when you need this practice most.

**Keep it simple enough so you'll actually do it. ✅**



### **Example: Ali’s Morning Manifesto**

- **Prime - Let’s connect to our body and prime our day with gratitude**

    - How are we feeling this morning?

    - We’re grateful for…

- **Align - Let’s remind ourselves of our key priorities**

    - What were our Quarterly Quests? How are they going?

    - What were our top 3 outcomes for this week? How are they going?

    - What’s today’s adventure going to be? Is it in the calendar?

- **Organise: Let’s make sure things are organised to avoid overwhelm**

    - When is today’s Mini Admin Party? Is it in the calendar?

    - Have we updated our Todo and Projects List?

    - Let’s review our Todo and Projects List. Based on that, what side quests will we complete today? Have we moved them to *Today* and blocked them in the calendar?

# Focus Time

## ⏳ Focus Time

> A mathematician can work... "four hours a day, or at most five, with breaks about every hour (for walks perhaps)" - John Edensor Littlewood

> Four hours creative work a day is about the limit for a mathematician. - G. H. Hardy

> I seemed to have psychic energy for only three or four hours of work, "real work", each day.ˮ - Paul Halmos

> All those I think who have lived as literary men - working daily as literary labourers - will agree with me that three hours a day will produce as much as a man ought to write. - Anthony Trollope

> Writers should... "spend three or four hours a day, four or five days a week, in a room with your computer, your characters, and your plot" - Laura Schellhardt

**❓How much should we** ***really*** **work each day?**

We should aim for 3-5 hours of genuine focused work each day. This doesn't mean meetings, checking emails, or Slack messages. It means genuine deep work where you're creating something or solving problems.

🎯**How much should you focus each day?**

- 3 hours per day = You're ahead of 90% of people

- 4 hours per day = You're ahead of 95% of people

- 5 hours per day = You're ahead of 99% of people

**☝️ Reminder**: Focus is a muscle which is built up over time and using systems which are personalized to your needs. For further resources, return to the Focus Hour Formula.



# Evening Shutdown

## 🌙 The Evening Shutdown

The Evening Shutdown is a few minutes at the end of your workday where you shut down "work mode" and boot up "life mode."

This idea comes from Cal Newport, who has been writing about it for ages (and whose work I absolutely love). While I'm just starting to implement this myself, the power of the Evening Shutdown is that it creates a clear boundary between work and personal life.



🌙 The Evening Shutdown is a **5-minute ritual with three components**:

- **Reflect**: A check in to see if our actions today aligned with the intentions set during the Morning Manifesto

- **Organise:** Updating tasks and projects list, flag any outstanding items

- **Shut Down**: Shifting to focus on life areas such as health, hobbies, and relationships 



**Example: Ali’s Evening Shutdown**

- **Reflect**

    - How did our actions today align with our intentions?

    - What were today's wins and learnings?

- **Organise**

    - Have we updated our Task and Projects List with any open loops?

    - Let's review our Task/Projects List and calendar. Any action items for tomorrow/this week?

- **Shut Down**

    - What could we do this evening for our Health?

    - What could we do this evening for our Hobbies?

    - What could we do this evening for our Relationships?
