# The Importance of Focused Hours

![image](../Images/Media/image%20(59).png)
[image - Notes](../Images/image%20(59).md)

Here's the framework I use for every focus session, which I like to call the Focused Hour Formula:

- Five minutes to plan and organise,

- Fifty minutes to focus, and

- Five minutes to reflect and recharge.

I've found that this 5-50-5 structure creates the perfect container for deep, meaningful work.

**The reason this works** so well is that research suggests most people can maintain deep focus for about 45-55 minutes before their concentration starts to waver.

The five minutes at either end act like brackets, helping you transition into and out of focus mode, while capturing any insights that might help improve your next session.

Sometimes I'll focus for less time, sometimes for more. It really depends on the task and how I'm feeling that day.

**The key is finding a rhythm that works for you** while maintaining the basic structure of preparation, focus, and reflection.

At the heart of meaningful progress lies the ability to focus deeply on important work.

## 🧑‍🍳 Phase 1: Plan & Organise

Before you start any focus session, take 5 minutes to align and organise. This might seem like unnecessary prep work, but it's actually crucial for maintaining focus once you begin.

Think of it like *mise en place* in cooking - having all your ingredients prepped and ready before you start makes the actual cooking process much smoother.



### 🎯**Plan:** Before a focus session, write down what you want to actually do during it.

This helps train your brain to be intentional and focused, rather than jumping in without direction. Keep it simple - just a single, clear objective that you can realistically achieve in one session.

This also might sound like common sense, but it’s definitely not common practice. I’ve had many times when I’ve started a focus session and I forgot to set my one intention. I'll start working on the computer, then I'm on Slack and checking emails, then I'm on YouTube because "it's market research" (I'm a YouTuber after all)... Before I know it, 29 minutes have passed and I haven’t actually gotten anything done because I didn’t make a plan for what I wanted to accomplish in the first place.

### 🗑️**Organise:** Take 1-2 minutes to declutter your workspace.

A cluttered desk leads to a cluttered mind. Clear away anything not essential for your current task like papers, devices, empty coffee cups. You'll find it much easier to maintain focus when your space is organised.

## ☕ Phase 2: Focus

![image](../Images/Media/image%20(61).png)
[image - Notes](../Images/image%20(61).md)

The heart of the Focused Hour Formula is, of course, the actual focus time. This is where you spend about 50 minutes working on your chosen task without letting yourself get distracted.

**The secret to focus is to** work on just *one* thing for an extended period of time.

If you really struggle with focusing for long, you can do 25 minutes and slowly work your way up. There are also some focus tools like [Opal](https://www.opal.so/) and [Forest](https://www.forestapp.cc/) that have gamified focus by blocking distractions from your phone.

# The Activation Menu

### ↗️ **Activation Menu:** The “on ramp” to focus

One way to **combat procrastination** is to create what I call an Activation Menu: a list of things that help you get started, sort of like an **"on ramp" to focus**.

The idea is to fill in this sentence: "When I notice I'm procrastinating, I'll do one or more of the following…"

These are some items from my Activation Menu:

- Put on my favourite ambient playlist

- Do a 3-minute breathing exercise to feel centred and alert

- Tackle a super easy admin task first to get into the mood

- Use [Voicepal](https://apps.apple.com/gb/app/voicepal-your-ai-ghostwriter/id6471552007), my ghostwriting assistant app, to talk out my plan for whatever I need to work on

- Change my location - the coffee shop downstairs often works

- Join a co-working session for accountability

# The Reactivation Menu

### 🔁 **Reactivation Menu:** Getting back on track

Similarly, **when you get distracted** during a focus session, it helps to have a Reactivation Menu to get back on track.

Fill in this prompt: "If I find myself getting distracted while focusing, I'll do one or more of the following…"

These are some items on my Reactivation Menu:

- Take a 60-second break to do some jumping jacks

- Switch up my music to *The Lord of the Rings* soundtrack

- Put my phone to *Do Not Disturb* and move it to another room

- Spend 2 minutes doing stretches in my chair

- Join a co-working session on Zoom for accountability

- Use [Voicepal](https://apps.apple.com/gb/app/voicepal-your-ai-ghostwriter/id6471552007) to dictate what I'm trying to work on

# Reflect & Recharge

## 💆 Phase 3: Reflect & Recharge

This is the final 5 minutes of our focused hour formula. Beyond just taking a well-earned break, this is your chance to learn from the session and make the next one even better.



![image](../Images/Media/image%20(62).png)
[image - Notes](../Images/image%20(62).md)



### 🪞**Reflect:** At the end of a focus session, ask yourself: “What could I tweak to make focusing easier next time?”

Small adjustments can often make a big difference. Keep a quick note of what helped or hindered your focus during the session. This practice of continuous improvement will help you optimise your focus environment over time.

**Ask yourself:**

- What worked well during this session?

- What kept pulling my attention away?

- How could I make the next session even better?

These are some examples on how small tweaks can have a big impact based on my reflections.

- **Example**: A box of tissues and a bin

    One small change that made a huge difference for me was adding a box of tissues to my desk. I used to get distracted whenever my nose was running because I'd have to get up and walk to the bathroom for toilet paper, which completely derailed my focus. I’ve since bought boxes of tissues and kept them on my desk. Now that's one less thing pulling me out of my flow state.

    Then the next day I realised I needed a place to put these tissues because I didn’t have a bin. So I went out and got a bin for my desk. My wife and I currently share a desk and we have a bin for random chocolate wrappers, orange peels, tissues, post-it notes, or whatever needs tossing in the middle of a deep work session.

- **Example:** Coffee and the bathroom

    I noticed that when I had black coffee, I'd constantly need to use the bathroom, which would completely break my concentration. So on days when I need extended periods of focus, I opt for tea instead.

### 🔋 **Recharge:** Take a few minutes to recharge your mental batteries.

This could mean stepping away from your desk, doing some stretches, or making a cup of tea. The key is to give your mind a chance to reset before diving into your next task. These short breaks between focus sessions are just as important as the focused work itself.

It’s helpful to come up with a Recharge Menu that genuinely helps you reset. It’s helpful to fill in the blanks with actions that you think might work for you: “When I’ve finished a work session or meeting, I’ll do one or more of the following to recharge my energy…”

These are some examples from my Recharge Menu:

- Go for a 10 minute walk outside and grab a coffee

- Use the bathroom while scrolling through Instagram Reels

- Mindfully make myself a nice cup of coffee using the Aeropress

- Do 5-10 minutes of stretches on the yoga mat

- Grab the guitar and play a couple of uplifting songs. Some personal favourites are *Take Me Home, Country Roads* by John Denver and *Castle on the Hill* by Ed Sheeran

- Chop and eat an apple with almond butter while lying on the bean bag



# Track Your Focus

## 📒 The Focus Log

In many ways, the single most impactful tool for improving your focus is keeping track of how long you actually spend in deep, focused work.

**Most of us think we're focusing for way longer than we actually are.** When I first started tracking my focused time, I was shocked to learn that in an 8-hour workday, I was only truly focused for about 2-3 hours. The rest was spent context switching, getting distracted, or doing shallow work that didn't really move the needle.

I use [Toggl](http://toggl.com/) for this (though we're building something even better called [Superfocus](https://superfocus.me/)), but you could use any time-tracking tool. The important thing is to be honest with yourself about what counts as focused time. If you're checking your phone or switching between tasks, it's not focused time.

> If the *only* change you made to your life was to consistently track your focus minutes, you'd probably double your productivity.

When you start paying attention to how much time you're actually spending in deep focus, you naturally start finding ways to increase that number.

> When you can measure what you are speaking about, and express it in numbers, you know something about it. - Lord Kelvin (British Mathematician)

## 🔬 Homework: Keep a Focus Log

For the next week, try tracking every minute you spend in focused work. This is a strategy known as self-monitoring, which is an evidence-based strategy that works well with behaviour change. It creates some natural feedback and can serve as a source of motivation.

Here's how to keep a focus log:

1. Download [Toggl](http://toggl.com/) (it's free) or use any timer you like

2. Start the timer whenever you begin focused work

3. Stop it when you get distracted or switch tasks

4. At the end of each day, note down your total focused time

5. Share your reflections in the comments section of this lesson

Aim to build up to 3–4 hours of focused work daily—this is the sweet spot for most people

# The Capture System

## 🪝 **Capture Method:** A “parking lot” for random thoughts

> Your brain is for having ideas. Not for holding them. - David Allen, *Getting Things Done*

For random thoughts that pop up during focus sessions, I keep a small notebook next to me. This way, I can quickly jot things down without letting them derail my focus. I used to try remembering everything, but I found that holding onto those thoughts actually made it harder to concentrate. Having a designated "parking lot" for random ideas helps maintain flow while giving you peace of mind that nothing important will be forgotten.

- **Example:** Analog using a notebook and pen

- **Example:** Digital using [Things 3](https://culturedcode.com/things/)
