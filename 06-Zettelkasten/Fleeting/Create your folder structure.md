## Finding the Right Structure

A well-defined structure for your notes can significantly improve how you capture, store, and retrieve information. This translates to faster note-taking, easier information recall, and a more efficient knowledge base.

While folders are a common organisational method, they aren't the only option. Ultimately, the best structure is the one that works for you. Here's how I personally organise my notes, but feel free to adapt it to your needs:

## To Create a New Note

> [!example]

1. Press `Ctrl+N` (or `Cmd+N` on macOS) on your keyboard.

2. Type the name of your note and press `Enter`.

3. Start typing your first note

> [!tip]

Start with a central location Note that you can keep coming back to like "Inbox", "Explore" or "Home". Make it fun to encourage growth. This will be your starting point every time you open your Vault. The purpose of this note is navigate you.

```markdown
Example of a Start Note
Start Note Name = Inbox, Home, Explore or START HERE
These will eventually become Maps of Contents (MOCS) which allow you to Index Notes within your Vault.
```
