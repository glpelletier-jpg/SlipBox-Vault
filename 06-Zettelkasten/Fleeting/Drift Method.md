# The Drift Method - Starter Guide

- Work shouldn’t feel like a fight. Most productivity systems promise clarity but create clutter. They push for more, faster — not better, lighter.

**The Drift Method** is different.

It’s not a rigid tool or fixed methodology. It’s a flexible system and mindset for moving work forward with clarity, direction, and ease.

This guide introduces the core ideas behind **The Drift Method** and gives you everything you need to begin building a system that flows. - ## **What is The Drift Method?**

**The Drift Method** is a system for structuring and evolving your work without over-planning or over-managing. It helps you: - Stay focused on what matters - Prioritize progress over pressure - Reflect, adapt, and grow without burning out

It works with any tool, any process, and any level of complexity — from solo projects to full teams.

Think of it as a set of building blocks that help you create **flow instead of friction**. - - ## **Core Principles** - ### 1. **Work should flow, not be forced**

Progress happens best when work moves smoothly. Remove friction, simplify where possible, and let direction guide momentum. - ### 2. **Direction over distraction**

Don’t do everything. Do what moves you toward your Beacons—the core aim or purpose of your current season or focus. - ### 3. **Clarity without clutter**

Forget endless dashboards and bloated tools. Keep only what’s essential and eliminate what drains your energy. - ### 4. **Flexible, not fixed**

The Drift Method bends to your style and tools. There’s no one right way—only ways that help you move forward. - ### 5. **Simple by default, extensible when needed**

Start light. Add structure only when it adds clarity. - ## **The 7 Disciplines**

The Drift Method is built on seven disciplines — modular, practical components that help you shape your system.

Use all of them, or start with what fits. Each one exists to create momentum without overwhelm. - ### 1. **Beacons**

Your guiding direction. These are your lodestars—the deeper reason or objective that your current work (and energy) funnels toward. - ### 2. **Flows**

Major streams of effort—projects, initiatives, or long-term work areas. Each Flow contains its own tasks, notes, and substructure. - ### 3. **Notes**

Ideas, insights, research, and feedback that support and shape each Flow. These help you capture context and learnings over time. - ### 4. **Steps**

Actionable units of progress. These are the things you do to move a Flow forward. Traditionally these might be called tasks or todos. - ### 5. **Subflows**

Nested workstreams within a Flow. Useful for organizing complexity without losing clarity. - ### 6. **Kits**

Reusable tools, templates, resources, and finished products. Keep things modular so you can build faster and smarter. - ### 7. **Views**

Lightweight ways to see progress, reflect, and decide what’s next—without micromanaging every detail. - ## Getting Started

You don’t need to implement everything at once. Start small: - **Choose a Beacon** – What direction matters most right now? - **Identify 1–2 Flows** – Choose projects or processes that need more clarity. - **Add a few Steps** – What actions will move each Flow forward? - **Capture a Note or two** – Jot down any ideas or context you’ll want to come back to. - You don’t need a perfect system. You need one that moves with you. The Drift Method is here to help you do just that.

Let your work flow.
