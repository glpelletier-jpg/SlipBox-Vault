## Literature Notes

⬆️:: Zettelkasten (../undefined)

---

> [!info] In the context of Obsidian, a "literature note" refers to a specific type of note that is used to capture and organise information from external sources, such as books, articles, research papers, or other published materials. Literature notes are usually stored in your [Vault] Folders

---

### The main purpose of a literature note is to:

1. Summarize the key ideas, findings, or arguments presented in the source material.

2. Capture relevant quotes, facts, or data that can be used for reference or further analysis.

3. Provide your own thoughts, reflections, or critiques on the content.

4. Make connections between the source material and your own ideas or other notes in your knowledge base.

---

### The structure of a literature note typically includes the following elements:

- **Bibliographic information:** The full citation or reference details of the source material.

- **Summary:** A concise overview of the main points, arguments, or conclusions presented in the source.

- **Highlights:** Relevant quotes, data, or key insights extracted from the source.

- **Thoughts and reflections:** Your own commentary, analysis, or personal connections to the content.

- **Links:** References to related notes, ideas, or concepts in your Obsidian vault.

---

By creating literature notes, you can effectively capture and organize the information you gather from your research, reading, or other sources. This helps you to better understand, synthesize, and apply the knowledge you acquire, rather than simply storing the source material itself.

Literature notes are particularly valuable in academic, research, or knowledge-intensive fields, where you need to engage deeply with a wide range of source materials. They can also be useful for personal learning, professional development, or any situation where you need to extract and integrate insights from various sources.
