# I almost gave up [Second Brain Quickstart]

At the age of 22, I developed a mysterious illness: an unexplained, debilitating pain in my throat.

At first, I thought it was a temporary thing. But it grew steadily worse, over months and then years.

I started seeing every kind of doctor and specialist I could find, undergoing every scan and test you can imagine. None of them could pin down the cause, much less provide a solution.

Finally, one of my doctors recommended a potential option: a powerful painkiller that might fix the problem.

​

But it came at a cost... the complete dulling of sensation throughout my entire body.

Considering such a drastic measure, **I realized that I was at rock bottom.**

Modern medicine had exhausted every possibility and I was left with just one. Numb the pain and live with it.

**I felt like I had no future**, that the things I wanted in my life – dating, friendships, a career I loved – were permanently out of reach because of this condition I couldn’t explain.

As I found myself sitting in my neurologist’s office, a realization – and resolution – slowly dawned on me.

I started to collect the notes from my countless patient records, doctor’s appointments, and my own research into the mysterious illness. I combed over my records, categorized, distilled, and created personalized resources summarizing the things that helped.

And finally, I ended up with a clear output, built from (and moving beyond) the raw, starting materials I assembled: a set of voice exercises, stress reduction techniques, and self-care rituals that allowed me to address the *root cause* of what my body was trying to tell me.

**This was the start of my Second Brain.**

I wanted to share this story with you because the lesson I learned is that the foundation for your Second Brain is *deeply personal*. Just as your reasons for wanting to improve your productivity are deeply personal.

It’s unique to you and your circumstances – and incorporates **your perspective and personal story**.

Building my Second Brain has allowed me to grow a business while maintaining my freedom and free time. It’s allowed me to take on big creative projects while staying balanced and healthy.

And during times of uncertainty and flux, a Second Brain allows me **to be present and to take each day one at a time.

​

​**The systems I’ve created are unique to me and act as a buffer against the uncertainties specific to my daily life. The chaos and complexity are contained in my Second Brain, which allows me to sleep peacefully at night.

Since building a Second Brain is such a personal endeavor, it makes sense to see how different each person’s journey can be.

**I’d like to share the perspective of one of our graduates from the Building a Second Brain program: an entrepreneur and YouTuber named Ali Abdaal.

​

​**Ali has over 4 million YouTube subscribers, and started multiple businesses while attending Cambridge medical school to become a doctor.

​

Ali uses *his* Second Brain to capture, organize, and create content and share it with the world in remarkable ways – but uses completely different notetaking tools than I do:

- At any given time, he’s used a combination of paper, Apple Notes, Notion, and Roam Research

- He’s used his Second Brain to write an upcoming book, YouTube scripts, plan podcasts, and do personal daily reflections

- Ali uses his company’s Second Brain to coordinate and plan out his team’s calendar of blog posts, videos, and other writing

    ​

While Ali might seem like a superhuman in some of his public achievements, there’s something interesting to notice about his practice...

First, he’s **inconsistent**.

Ali uses a gamut of different notetaking tools, instead of one consistent tool.

​

He switches his daily practice fairly often and isn’t always able to complete “weekly reviews.”

And sometimes, he ignores his “Second Brain” entirely during crunch periods.

But despite all this seemingly sub-optimal notetaking behavior… **he’s radically prolific**. How?

Despite all the superficial productivity rules Ali breaks, he’s following a **deeper system**.

​

A system that’s flexible, consistent, and allows him to personalize his note-taking practice to his current life priorities – instead of forcing his life into an arbitrary framework he can’t possibly maintain.

This is the beauty of the Second Brain method of notetaking, and a direct result of the Forte Labs team slowly refining it over the course of 5 years and 15 online cohorts, alongside thousands of highly motivated, top-performing students like Ali.

We all need a system that is *personal*, *practical*, and has been *battle-tested* by real people like us. Not an idealized, perfect system (that breaks the second your life gets busy).

​

​

(Just like Ali, myself, and thousands of others are doing every day.)
