# Your Life Compass

## Introduction to 🧭 Life Compass

Most of us are really good at being busy: We tick off tasks, meet deadlines, and keep pushing forward. But sometimes we look up and wonder, "Is all of this work actually getting me where I want to go?" The

**Life Compass helps to define your true North Star**, giving you a direction on life that can fill your days with clarity and purpose.

## 🥜 In a Nutshell

The Life Compass helps you define your true North Star by exploring your values and long-term aspirations through four exercises:

- **The Eulogy Method**: Imagining what people would say at your funeral to understand your core values

- **The Bucket List**: Dreaming without limits about what you’d like to do, create, and experience

- **The Mission Prompt**: Discovering how you'd serve others if money weren’t a concern

- **The Success Prompt**: Defining what true contentment means to you personally

👉 Result: An always-evolving document to help align your actions with your values

Your Life Compass is your guiding star, rooted in your values and long-term aspirations.

It helps you answer big questions like, “What kind of life do I want to live?” and “What really matters to me?”.

Your North Star doesn’t necessarily have to be your destination — and it can change over time — but having one will **give you a sense of direction based on** your core values, your desired legacy and the impact you want to create.

> Sometimes we are knocked out of our left brain environment and thought patterns and into the right brain by an unplanned experience. The death of a loved one, a severe illness, a financial setback, or extreme adversity can cause us to stand back, look at our lives, and ask ourselves some hard questions: “What’s really important? Why am I doing what I’m doing?” But if you’re proactive, you don’t have to wait for circumstances or other people to create perspective-expanding experiences. You can consciously create your own. - Stephen Covey, *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People*



📚 The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People



Stephen’s point is that you can think of where you want your life to go without having to through a major crisis with a friend or a loved one. This is one of the absolute critical pillars of productivity because there’s nothing sadder than someone who’s super efficient and super productive, but who spends years working towards something only to realise that they were actually working towards the wrong things.

## 💡 **Getting the most out of these exercises**

These are several exercises that can give you clarity and help you point to your North Star, which we’ll walk through below. Here are some tips to get you started:

- **Find your space**: Step away from your usual environment—a quiet coffee shop, a park bench or any place where you won't be interrupted

- **Set aside quality time**: Give yourself 1-2 hours of uninterrupted reflection time

- **Create the right atmosphere**: Put on inspiring music that helps you think deeply

- **Turn off notifications**: This is your time to think without distractions

- **Write it out**: There is some evidence that writing the answers to these exercises helps your brain to think a little bit more creatively

# Your Eulogy

## 🪦 **The Eulogy Method**

Imagine you've lived to 100, having enjoyed great physical, mental and spiritual health. You're observing your own funeral from above. What would you want people to say about how you lived?

**Reflect on:**

- What would your family say about how you showed up in their lives?

- What would friends remember about your friendship and the memories you created?

- What would colleagues say about your contributions to your field?

- What would your community highlight about your impact?



💡The Eulogy Method


**🌟 Example: Ali’s Eulogy Prompt**

I’m attending my own funeral, aged 100. This is what people are saying about me.

- Izzy: Kind, loving, present husband. Inspiring and kind to everyone he met. With all the success he had sharing his message with people around the world, his family always came first.

- Children: Awesome dad, always supportive and inspiring, helped us feel like we could do anything, and that he and mum would always love and support us with whatever we wanted to do. We’d go to him when struggling with anything, and he’s always be there to provide a listening ear, and counsel (when asked). Fun, funny, sincere, humble. The dining table was always full, the house was always full of laughter and love.

- Taimur: We continued our weekly (ish) podcast episodes for decades. Talked about anything and everything.

- Friends: Open door policy, house always full of laughter and love. Ali loved to bring people together, to organise events, to make people feel welcome. Wise, friendly, kind.

- Colleagues: Major impact on helping people improve their lives, figure out what was meaningful to them, live more intentionally, not waste our lives. Gracious and supportive with every team member, cultivated an energising and inspiring atmosphere at work that made you look forward to Mondays.

- Community: Always organising events to bring people together, open door policy at the house and office, friends and community members were always welcome to hang out, Ali was always there when someone needed a listening ear.

## 🪣 The Bucket List

💡The Bucket List

**The goal is to write down as much stuff as you possibly can** in any of these different categories. We’re going for quantity rather than quality. Hopefully this will reveal something interesting about our motivations, desires and aspirations.

# Your Mission

## 🚀 The Mission Prompt

You’re in a magical world where you’ve got all the time and money in the world and you’re not afraid of failure or what other people will think of you. The only catch is that you have to spend 40 hours a week using your talents to serve other people in this world.

This prompt is trying to get at your core motivations and purpose by removing practical constraints like money. This hopefully reveals your authentic calling and what brings you deep fulfilment.

💡The Mission Prompt


### Example: Ali’s Mission Prompt

In this world, I might use my talents to serve others in the following ways

- Creating amazing apps / tools that help people make the most of their time / live more intentionally / improve their lives

- Tour the world doing Tony Robbins-esque stage shows / seminars that help people unlock their limitations and dream bigger, more intentionally etc

- Write books in the self-help category (although some would be fiction)

- Write songs that have an inspiring message behind them

    - Maybe even just write songs that move people emotionally

- Host IRL workshops and events that bring people together

- Continue making YouTube videos when I had stuff to share

- Read ancient wisdom + philosophy etc, trial stuff on myself, and make the knowledge and advice accessible to a mass modern audience

- Illustrated books with a self help-y message a la Charlie Mackesy that I illustrate myself

- Create my own home-schooling method / platform

- Build education platform that helps anyone in the world learn high-income skills



# Your Definition of Success

## ⭐ The Success Prompt

Take a moment to imagine yourself in a state of complete contentment. Not the rush of achievement or the glow of recognition, but genuine, lasting peace. In this state of deep satisfaction with your life:

- What fills your days?

- Who surrounds you?

- What impact are you having?

- What worries are absent?

**Complete these statements by focusing on what truly matters to you**, *not what others value*. Write from your heart, not your resume. Think about what would make you feel fulfilled even if no one else ever knew about it.



💡The Success Prompt


### Example: Ali’s Success Prompt

For me, a successful life would be one where I’ve enjoyed the passage of time, I’ve contributed to the world in some small way, I’ve been connected to the people and to the world around me, I’ve grown and flourished as a person,I’ve built a loving family, I’ve cultivated connections with the people around me, I’ve savoured what this life has to offer.For me,success really means to be spending my time exactly how I’d like to be spending it. Intentionally. That means being free to do whatever I want, whenever I want, and choosing to invest that freedom into commitments like family, work, people, connections, growth.I’ll be proud of how I’ve lived my life if I’ve enjoyed the passage of time, contributed to the world in some way, built loving relationships, been a force  for positivity and love for the people around me and the people I serve through my work.The glow of genuine peace and contentment:

- Loving family

- Peaceful

- Lots of reading and learning

- Writing

- Teaching IRL

- Working with people IRL

- Racket sports with friends

- Hosting people
