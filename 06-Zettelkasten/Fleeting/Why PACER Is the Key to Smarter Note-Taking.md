## Why PACER Is the Key to Smarter Note-Taking

title:: Why PACER Is the Key to Smarter Note-Taking producer:: Spencer Abrahams input:: [Authors](../People/Authors.csv) source:: Readwise import link:: [https://medium.com/p/2ba5a0cd14e9\](https://medium.com/p/2ba5a0cd14e9%5C) tags::

> Developed by Dr. Justin Sung as a learning framework, PACER categorizes information into five distinct types:

> Analogous information is all about comparison. It’s when you’re trying to understand how different approaches stack up against each other. Like when you’re figuring out whether plain text notes or that fancy new app will actually work better for you.

> Procedural stuff is anything you need to actually do to learn. Think riding a bike or learning to code. You can’t just read about it — you have to practice it. Reading about riding a bike won’t keep you from falling off.

> Conceptual information? That’s your big-picture stuff. The theories and principles that explain why things work the way they do. Like understanding why linking ideas together helps you remember them better.

> Evidence is exactly what it sounds like — the proof that something works (or doesn’t). Your real-world examples and case studies. The screenshots of that perfect note organization system that actually stuck around for more than a week
