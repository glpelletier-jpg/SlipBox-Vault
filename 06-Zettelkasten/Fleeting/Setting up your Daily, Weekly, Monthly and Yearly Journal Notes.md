> [!quote] Before creating my Second Brain, I mindlessly consumed information without ever stopping to reflect. This passive approach overloaded my mind, leading to burnout and difficulty recalling what I'd learned. Journaling emerged as a solution, prompting me to actively engage with the information I encountered.

> [!info]- Why Journaling is so powerful Journaling is considered one of the most powerful tools for personal growth and transformation due to several key benefits:

1. **Increases self-awareness and emotional intelligence.** By writing about your thoughts, feelings, and experiences, you gain deeper insight into your inner world, patterns, triggers, and motivations. This heightened self-awareness leads to greater emotional intelligence and understanding of yourself

2. **Provides an outlet for processing emotions.** Journaling allows you to express and work through difficult emotions like anger, grief, or anxiety in a healthy way. This emotional release can reduce stress and improve mood

3. **Improves mental health.** Numerous studies show journaling can decrease symptoms of depression, anxiety, and PTSD. It helps break negative thought cycles, regulate emotions, and increase mindfulness

4. **Boosts memory and comprehension.** The act of writing things down engages different brain regions and enhances working memory capacity, comprehension, and cognitive processing.

5. **Fosters creativity and problem-solving.** Journaling, especially stream-of-consciousness writing, taps into your right brain and unlocks creative insights. It allows you to explore different perspectives on challenges.

6. **Promotes goal achievement.** Writing about your goals and tracking progress increases motivation and accountability, making you more likely to follow through.

7. **Provides a record of personal growth.** Re-reading past journal entries allows you to see how far you've come and gain perspective on your life's journey.

8. **Offers a safe space for self-expression.** Journals provide a judgment-free zone to freely explore your innermost thoughts, feelings, and desires without fear.

By Default Obsidian comes with a core plugin called [Daily Notes](https://help.obsidian.md/Plugins/Daily+notes). This plugin opens a note based on todays date and creates it if it doesn't already exist. The best use case for a Daily Note is a Journal, To-Do List or Daily thoughts or brain dumps that you discover throughout your day.

I would recommend skipping [Daily Notes](https://help.obsidian.md/Plugins/Daily+notes) and installing a community plugin called [Periodic Notes](https://github.com/liamcain/obsidian-periodic-notes). The plugin was developed by Liam Cain who is part of the Obsidian Core Development Team.

This works well alongside the [Calendar Plugin](https://github.com/liamcain/obsidian-calendar-plugin) which is also developed by Liam Cain.

# Setting up your Daily, Weekly, Monthly and Yearly Journal Notes

## Step 1 - Install the Periodic Notes Community Plugin

> [!info] Settings > Community Plugins > Browse > Periodic Notes > Install > Enable Once you have enabled the plugin, click on the "Options"

> [!example] [image: Calendar-Plugin-Periodic-Notes-SS.png]

---

## Step 2 - Open Plugin Options

> [!info]

1. Select Periodic Notes

1. Enter a preferred Date Time Format your notes will be created in

2. Set a Daily Note Template, I have included one in the kit but feel free to use your own.

3. This will be the default folder Daily Notes will be placed in. This keeps everything nice and organised.

4. Turn on Weekly Notes

5. Set a Weekly Note Template, I have included one in the kit but feel free to use your own.

6. This will be the default folder Weekly Notes will be placed in. This keeps everything nice and organised.

7. If you want to set up Monthly Notes repeat the processes above

8. If you want to set up Quaterly Notes repeat the processes above

9. If you want to set up Yearly Notes repeat the processes above

> [!example] [image: Obsidian-Community-Plugins-PNotes-Options-SS.png]

---

## Step 3 - Install the Calendar Plugin

Settings > Community Plugins > Browse > Calendar > Install > Enable In Options choose the start of the weekday. By Default it's Sunday.

> [!example] [image: Calendar-Plugin-SS.png]

> [!info]

1. Left Click and Drag Calendar to the bottom of the bottom Right Sidebar then release the mouse button.
