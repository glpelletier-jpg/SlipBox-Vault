# How to Use Readwise



# Getting to Know Readwise



## Introduction to How to Use Readwise



What is this book, How to Use Readwise? It's our version of a user manual, but it's built into Readwise. These highlights will be resurfaced automatically like those from any other book, and because it's within Readwise, that means you can perform all the standard actions. You can make this book appear more or less often or not at all, you can favorite (to see the highlight more often) and discard (to no longer see the highlight), and so on. We sincerely hope these tips help you get more out of Readwise! If not, you can stop this book from resurfacing at any time.



## Remember More with the Daily Review



Readwise gives you a couple of ways to easily revisit your highlights. The first is the Daily Review email, intended as a casual way to resurface knowledge—perhaps while drinking your morning coffee or riding the train into work. The second is the app (available on [web](https://readwise.io/dashboard), [iOS](https://readwise.app.link/m88AXbvcygb), and [Android](https://readwise.app.link/E0AsXt7lIgb)), where you can dig into processing and summarizing your learnings by adding notes and tags to your highlights.



## How to Use the Readwise Dashboard



The [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard) is the command center of your Readwise experience. From here, you can access your Daily Review, add more highlight sources (including Kindle, Apple Books, Kobo, Medium, Snipd, and Pocket, among many others), view highlights by book or article, customize your preferences, search your library, edit or create Themed Reviews, and more.



## How to View the Highlights of a Book



You can view all your highlights for any given book by selecting [Books](https://readwise.io/library) from the Readwise Dashboard. From the Books view, you can see statistics for each book, such as how many highlights you've taken and the date of your last highlight, and you can perform actions such as increasing or decreasing the frequency of seeing a particular book in your Daily Review. You can do the same for highlights from any other type of content (e.g. articles, tweets, and podcasts).



## How to Review a Specific Book



The cornerstone of Readwise is the Daily Review, but many users also like to review their highlights from a single book from start to finish. Reviewing your highlights shortly after finishing a book is one of the best ways to solidify all your takeaways from that book. If you have too many highlights to "process" in one sitting, Readwise remembers where you left off so you can pick up the process in a future session. To review your highlights from a specific book or article in chronological order, navigate to it from the [Books](https://readwise.io/library) section of Readwise.



## How to View and Review Specific Articles



In addition to books, you can also review highlights from articles that you’ve read and highlighted in reading apps such as Instapaper, Pocket, or Medium. We’re even building our own [reading app](https://readwise.io/read), which shares a database with Readwise and seamlessly syncs all highlights from one to the other. Just like viewing Books, you access your Articles by clicking the [Articles](https://readwise.io/articles) link on the Readwise Dashboard.



## How to Manage Your Readwise Account



The [Account Settings](https://readwise.io/preferences/account) menu is where you can modify your subscription, update your billing info, export all your data, and delete your account. You can reach your account settings via the Readwise Dashboard. **Note: If you delete your account, all your data will be permanently wiped from our servers and cannot be recovered.**

## How Readwise Began



Many users ask us about how Readwise got started. It began in 2016 when [Tristan Homsi](https://twitter.com/homsiT) and [Daniel Doyon](https://twitter.com/deadly_onion) connected through an obscure comment about reading lists on Hacker News and subsequently bonded over a mutual interest in so-called "reading tech". One thing led to another and they decided to collaborate on an MVP, which launched in May 2017. Readwise has organically evolved ever since. In 2021, we began working on Reader, a power user's read-it-later app. You can learn more about the launch of Reader on our blog: [The Next Chapter of Readwise: Our Own Reading App](https://blog.readwise.io/readwise-reading-app/).



## How Readwise is Funded



Some users ask us why we've decided to bootstrap Readwise through revenues rather than raise venture capital. It's a complicated answer to a complicated question, but long story short, we concluded that bootstrapping would better enable us to focus on our small niche market of "nonfiction power readers" rather than having to pursue the larger but more casual venture scale market. You can read more about this decision here: [Why We're Bootstrapping Readwise](https://blog.readwise.io/why-were-bootstrapping-readwise/).



# Synchronizing Your Data



## How to Automatically Sync Your Kindle Highlights



Any highlights you take in a Kindle ebook are automatically synchronized to the Amazon cloud — but only if you purchased the ebook directly from Amazon. Readwise uses a browser extension to automatically save these Kindle highlights to your Readwise account and keep them synced. Please note that you have to use Chrome or Firefox with the Readwise extension installed for Kindle highlights to sync automatically. Otherwise, you'll need to use the mobile app to manually sync your Kindle highlights from time to time.



## How to Manually Sync Your Kindle Highlights



If you don't use Chrome or Firefox with the Readwise extension installed, you'll need to manually "resync" your Kindle highlights from time to time. The easiest way to resync is to use the mobile app, tap into the **Add Highlights** section, and tap **Sync** next to the Kindle option. You can also [upload the MyClippings.txt](https://readwise.io/import_clippings) file directly from your Kindle device.
Readwise will send you a reminder email every time you’ve gone 45 days without a new highlight, as a gentle nudge to resync.



## How to Sync Your Apple Books Highlights



In addition to Amazon Kindle, Readwise supports a variety of other reading platforms, including Apple Books. You can import your Apple Books highlights into Readwise by selecting [Import](https://readwise.io/welcome/sync) from the Readwise Dashboard and then clicking the Apple Books option. The easiest method for syncing requires being on a Mac computer and downloading our Mac tool, but you can also [send highlights directly from mobile](https://help.readwise.io/article/35-how-do-i-import-apple-books-highlights-from-my-iphoneipad) on a per-book basis.



## How to Supplement Your Account with Popular Highlights



If you want to use Readwise but don’t have enough highlights of your own, or if you’ve read a paper book or listened to an audiobook where you couldn’t take highlights, you can *supplement* your account with popular highlights from those books. Even though this isn’t the same as revisiting your own highlights, it's surprisingly effective! You can add popular highlights by selecting [Import](https://readwise.io/welcome/sync) from the Readwise Dashboard and then clicking the [Supplemental Highlights](https://readwise.io/quick) option.



## How to Manually Sync Your Kindle Highlights Using My Clippings.txt



Every highlight you make on your Kindle device is saved to a local text file called My Clippings.txt. You can import the highlights from this file into Readwise by clicking the [Import](https://readwise.io/welcome/sync) option from the Readwise Dashboard and then clicking the [My Clippings.txt](https://readwise.io/import_clippings) option. This technique is useful if you don’t wish to use the Readwise browser extension or if you’re dealing with highlights from documents you did *not* purchase from Amazon.



## How to Sync Your Pocket Highlights



Readwise isn't limited to just books: you can also import highlights from articles. There are numerous reading apps out there (including [our own reading app](https://readwise.io/read)), but one of the most popular is Pocket. You can connect your Pocket account to Readwise by selecting [Import](https://readwise.io/welcome/sync) from the Readwise Dashboard and clicking the Pocket link.



## How to Manually Add Highlights



In addition to syncing highlights from a variety of digital reading platforms, you can also manually type or paste your highlights into Readwise. To create a book in Readwise and begin adding highlights, select [Import](https://readwise.io/welcome/sync) from the Readwise Dashboard and click the [Freeform Input](https://readwise.io/import_freeform) link.



## How to Save Tweets and Twitter Threads



Twitter can be a profound source of wisdom — just like books and articles — filled with insights worth revisiting. You can save both individual tweets and whole Twitter threads to Readwise. To begin saving from Twitter, first connect your Twitter account to Readwise by selecting [Import](https://readwise.io/welcome/sync) from the Readwise Dashboard and clicking the [Twitter](https://readwise.io/twitter_start) link. Once connected, you can publicly save individual tweets by replying on Twitter with “@readwiseio save”. You can privately save tweets by DMing the tweet to the @readwiseio account. To save whole threads, you can reply with “@readwiseio save thread”.



## How to Manually Import Highlights in Bulk Using CSV



If you have highlights from an obscure source, such as a niche reading or note-taking app, you can often import those into Readwise using a CSV file (comma separated values). You can upload a CSV file by selecting [Import](https://readwise.io/welcome/sync) from the Readwise Dashboard and clicking the [CSV Import](https://readwise.io/import_bulk) link. From there, you will need to follow the instructions and examples on how to properly structure your data.



# Customizing the Daily Review



## How to Change the Readwise Email Frequency



By default, you'll receive a Daily Review email once per day. You can change this preference to be every other day, weekly, or never by selecting [Email Preferences](https://readwise.io/preferences) from the Readwise Dashboard. From there, you can also change the email address and the time the email is sent.



## How Email Frequency Adjusts Automatically



During busy times, it can be a little overwhelming to watch emails pile up in your inbox. If you’re unable to read the Daily Review email for several days in a row, Readwise will automatically decrease the frequency at which emails are sent. First it will decrease from every day to every other day, then from every other day to every week. It works the same in reverse. Once you start reading your emails again, your frequency will be automatically increased. (Note that once you've manually set a review time, that will overrule this!)



## How to Customize Email Send Time



By default, you're set to receive your Daily Review email at 8:00 AM Eastern Standard Time. For many users, this morning routine is perfect. If you're in a different time zone, or if Readwise fits better into a different part of your routine, you can change this send time by selecting the [Email Preferences](https://readwise.io/preferences) menu from the Readwise Dashboard and toggling the Send Time option. (If your Daily Review isn't arriving when you think it should be, make sure you've set the correct time zone! You can click on the gray text to the right of the time dropdown to select one.)



## How to Change the Daily Review Length



By default, you'll receive 5 highlights per Daily Review. You can increase or decrease this number by selecting [Review Settings](https://readwise.io/configure/review) from the Readwise Dashboard. From there, you can change how many highlights you want to receive in each review, as well as other options like whether you want your Daily Reviews to favor older or newer highlights.



## How to Disable the Quality Filter



By default, your Daily Review will exclude highlights that appear to be short sentence fragments. Readwise does this because many users accidentally take incomplete highlights when just starting with a reading platform such as Kindle, and these fragments can be underwhelming to revisit. If you want to see all your highlights included in Daily Reviews, you can disable this setting by selecting [Review Settings](https://readwise.io/configure/review) from the Readwise Dashboard and toggling the Highlight Quality Filter option.



## How to See Newer or Older Highlights



If you’ve been reading and highlighting ebooks for several years, you may have tens of thousands of highlights that reflect your changing interests over time. By default, your Daily Review will randomly select from the highlights with no adjustment for how new or old a highlight is, but you can tweak the resurfacing algorithm to favor newer or older highlights by selecting [Review Settings](https://readwise.io/configure/review) from the Readwise Dashboard and moving the Highlight Recency slider.



## How to Exclude Specific Books



By default, your Daily Review will randomly select highlights from all documents contained in your library. If there’s a document you no longer want to revisit, you can exclude it from future Daily Reviews by visiting your [Library](https://readwise.io/everything), clicking the down arrow for the document in question, and then sliding the Review Frequency to “Never”. You can also conveniently disable a document *during* a Daily Review by selecting the down arrow in the upper right corner and clicking "Never show me this book again".

# Basic Features



## Favoriting



If you see a highlight you particularly love or that you'd like to make easily retrievable, you can "favorite" it. To favorite a highlight in the Daily Review email, click the Favorite link underneath a given highlight. To favorite a highlight in the Readwise web app, click the heart icon or use the keyboard shortcut **F**. You can view all these favorited highlights at any time by selecting [Favorites](https://readwise.io/favorites) from the [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard). Note: Once you have enough, you'll start receiving a special Daily Review on Sundays composed only of your favorite highlights.



## Discarding



Let's be honest: Many of our highlights are not the best. Maybe we accidentally highlighted a fragment because of Kindle's annoying refresh rate. Maybe we highlighted a passage that made sense in the moment but doesn't mean much without context. Whatever the reason, if Readwise resurfaces a highlight that's unlikely to have any future value to you, you should "discard" it. You can discard highlights from the Readwise email by clicking the Discard link underneath each highlight. You can discard in the Readwise web app by clicking the Discard button or using the keyboard shortcut **D**.
*Note: Discarded highlights are not permanently deleted. You can retrieve them any time by selecting* [Discards](https://readwise.io/discards) *from the* [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard)*.*



## Tagging



Tags are a great way to organize your highlights by topic, keyword, or any other use case you might have. You can tag highlights in the Daily Review email by clicking the Tag link underneath each highlight. You can tag them in the Readwise web app by clicking "Add tags" or using the keyboard shortcut **T**. You can then browse highlights on a tag-by-tag basis by selecting [Tags](https://readwise.io/tags) from the [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard). (You can also add tags while you read using a special Readwise feature called [Inline Tagging](https://blog.readwise.io/tag-your-highlights-while-you-read/)!)



## Noting



We highly recommend taking notes alongside your highlights. After all, the best way to read between the lines is to write between the lines. Any notes you take in Kindle, Apple Books, Instapaper, or elsewhere will be automatically imported to Readwise and attached to the respective highlight. You can edit this note in the Readwise web app by clicking the note icon, clicking into the body of the note itself, or using the keyboard shortcut **N**. Of course, you can also add a new note to any highlight that doesn't have one already.



## Sharing to Twitter or Facebook



It's easy to share particularly salient highlights on Twitter and Facebook through Readwise. Simply click the Share link underneath the noteworthy highlight in the Daily Review email or click the share icon in the Readwise web app and follow the instructions from there. (Pro tip: you can also use the keyboard shortcut **S**). Readwise will generate a "quoteshot" (an image instead of plain text) to which you can add your own commentary. We recommend tagging the original author to give them a little boost as well.



## Copying Highlight to Clipboard



Want to quickly copy and paste a highlight for use elsewhere? Perhaps in an article you're writing, a Slack discussion, or a Twitter debate? Simply click the down arrow in the upper right of each highlight and select "Copy highlight text". Pro tip: You can also use the keyboard shortcuts **CC** and **CX** to copy a highlight to the clipboard.



## Opening in Kindle App



Many times a highlight resurfaced in Readwise won't be enough by itself and you'll want to return to the highlight in the context of the book. You can automatically launch the Kindle app (if it's installed) and open the book to the appropriate location by clicking the down arrow in the upper right of the highlight and selecting "Open this book in Kindle". Note that due to technical limitations, this only works on desktop (not mobile) and only from the web app (not email).
Pro tip: You can also do this for highlights saved from [Reader](https://read.readwise.io/), and that works on web *and* mobile!



## Processing Highlights



The first time you see a highlight resurfaced in Readwise, it will have a blue dot in the upper left corner. This indicates that the highlight is still "unprocessed" similar to an unread email. Once you read the highlight or perform an action, the highlight will then be considered "processed". This is helpful for readers wishing to systematically review all their highlights, either in whole or for particular books. You can also mouseover the dot to see additional data, such as when you originally took the highlight.



## Using Keyboard Shortcuts in the Web App



Readwise is built for power readers, so the web app has also been designed for power users. This means, of course, that we have all kinds of keyboard shortcuts. There's not enough room to list them all here, but almost all shortcuts are discoverable if you hover over an action in the web app.



## Searching



One of the nice things about having all your highlights synchronized in a single repository is that it's easy to retrieve the excerpt you're looking for. You can hunt for a particular highlight by browsing the [Book](https://readwise.io/library) or [Article](https://readwise.io/articles), by using your [Tags](https://readwise.io/tags), or by using good old-fashioned search. A search box is displayed in the upper right corner of most Readwise screens. Alternatively, you can go to the [Search](https://readwise.io/search) page to query your entire Readwise catalog.
*Note: The search options outlined above are* ***full-text*** *and* ***tag*** *search, but the small search box found above the list of documents in your* [Library](https://readwise.io/everything) *searches* ***document title*** *and* ***author*** *only.*



## Bonus Highlight



At the end of every Daily Review, you'll discover a "bonus highlight" from a book recommended specifically for you. Our recommendation algorithm uses the books you've actually read, and the number of highlights you've made in each of those books, to predict books we think you'll actually love. In contrast, most online retailers try to predict books they think you'll buy. The bonus highlight is a feature and we do not make any affiliate revenue off of these links. You can turn off the bonus highlight any time by selecting [Preferences](https://readwise.io/preferences) from the [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard) and toggling the Bonus Highlights option.
*Note: Bonus highlights share space with these Readwise Tips. If both are enabled, the extra highlight at the end of your Daily Review will be randomly selected from the combined pool of bonus highlights and tips. If you only want to see bonus highlights in this space, you can tune down the frequency of the* ***How to Use Readwise*** *book.*



# Advanced Usage



## Scroll Mode versus Review Mode



The Readwise web app has two views which we call "Scroll Mode" and "Review Mode". Scroll Mode displays highlights vertically. This is great for quick scanning, juxtaposing highlights, or using **Cmd/ctrl + F** to search for a term. Review Mode displays highlights one at a time in a horizontal view, similar to the Daily Review. This is great for focusing all your attention on a single passage and taking actions — such as tagging, noting, or spaced repetition — before moving on to the next. You can switch between these views by toggling the button in the upper right corner of the Readwise web app or using the keyboard shortcut **LL**.



## Readwise Resurfacing Algorithm



It's not uncommon for a Readwise user to have more than 10,000 highlights in their catalog. If this user reviews 10 highlights per Daily Readwise, it'd take nearly three years to go through them all (assuming no new highlights are made during that time!). Because of this, we've built a "resurfacing algorithm" which tries to show you the "right highlight at the right time" — kind of like Spotify trying to serve up daily mixes based on your listening history. The Readwise algorithm also enables you to take control and manually "tune" many of its settings. You can do this by selecting the [Preferences](https://readwise.io/preferences) menu from the [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard) and toggling the various settings.



## Book Tuning



Is there a particular book that pops up a little too much in your Daily Reviews? Or maybe there's a book that you wish you saw more often? You can increase or decrease the frequencies of each book or article using a feature called "tuning". Simply select the [Preferences](https://readwise.io/preferences) menu from the [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard) and click into the [Frequency Tuning](https://readwise.io/configure/tune/books) tab. For users with many documents, this is one of the most powerful features in Readwise.
Pro tip: You can also increase or decrease the frequency of a particular document during your Daily Review by using the dropdown menu in the top right.



## Mid-Review Book Tuning



Do you sometimes come across a profound highlight in your Daily Review that makes you wish that book were resurfaced more? Good news! You can instruct Readwise to "Show this book more often" by clicking the down arrow in the upper right corner of the web app (you can also use the keyboard shortcut **+**). Going forward, the probability of this book's highlights resurfacing will be increased. You can tell Readwise to "Show this book less often" (keyboard shortcut **-**) or even "Never show this book again".



## Why Retention?



Why does Readwise focus so much on retention? For the same reasons that founders should reduce churn in a SaaS business before investing in growth, investors should buffer their portfolios against losses before seeking gains, or bodybuilders should prevent muscle loss before adding lean mass. No matter what you’re doing, the best way to grow is to first prevent losses.
Think of it this way: If you've got a leaky bucket, you're better off fixing the leak before pouring water in the top. For those of you who prefer numbers: If you start with a portfolio worth $100 and lose 50%, you now need to earn 100% to breakeven. This principle also extends to learning, and spaced repetition is a great way to patch your bucket or make up your losses.



## Spaced Repetition Basics



Readwise helps you remember more of what you read using two principles borrowed from cognitive science: [spaced repetition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaced_repetition) and [active recall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_recall). Spaced repetition is a scientifically proven method for efficient learning that progressively increases the time between reviews of previously learned material. Basically, we remember things better if we spread our reviews out over time rather than cramming. Reading a book is kind of like cramming — we intensely learn about a single subject for a few weeks, then we move onto the next book. Readwise uses your highlights to space out each book you've read into perpetuity. The result is that you retain profoundly more of what you read.



## Spaced Repetition Feedback Buttons



When you start using Readwise, its proprietary [spaced repetition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaced_repetition) algorithm assumes that each of your highlights is of equal quality, equal relevance, and equal difficulty. For most people, this is not entirely true. Some highlights are better than average, others are below. Some highlights are especially relevant right now, others not so much. Some highlights are hard to remember, others are easy.
You can take control of this algorithm by supplying feedback in the web app. If a highlight is better than average, especially relevant, or difficult, instruct Readwise "Soon" (keyboard shortcut **2**), and you'll see this highlight more often than average. If a highlight is in the middle, instruct Readwise "Later" (keyboard shortcut **3**). You get the idea. If you consistently supply feedback, you'll watch the quality of your reviews steadily improve. In turn, you'll retain significantly more of what you read.



## Active Recall (Mastery)



Readwise helps you remember more of what you read using two principles borrowed from cognitive science: [spaced repetition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaced_repetition) and [active recall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_recall). Active recall (also known as quizzing, testing, or retrieval) is the process by which we challenge our minds to retrieve a piece of information. Simply rereading a passage from time to time, on the other hand, is passive. Rereading is no doubt better than never revisiting the passage, but research has repeatedly shown that active recall is significantly more effective. So how can you take advantage of active recall in Readwise? For any highlight that you wish to deliberately commit to memory, hit the Master icon in the web app (keyboard shortcut **M**). You can then convert that highlight from a passive quote to an active flashcard. In turn, you'll retain vastly more of what you read.



## Cloze Deletion (Mastery)



Readwise offers increasing degrees of active recall intensity (Mastery). The least demanding is a deceptively simple technique called [cloze deletion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloze_test), more commonly known as "fill in the blank". With cloze deletion, a salient keyword is hidden from the passage, giving you an opportunity to pause and actively recall the missing information. This might sound trivial, but the simple act forces you to focus on the surrounding context and search your mind. This effort, in turn, is scientifically proven to form stronger memories enabling you to retain profoundly more of what you've read. To apply cloze deletion to a highlight, hit the Master icon in the web app (keyboard shortcut **M**).



## Question & Answer (Mastery)



Readwise offers increasing degrees of active recall intensity (Mastery). The least demanding is cloze deletion, but the most powerful is question & answer. Q&A is exactly what it sounds like: You convert a passage containing a piece of wisdom into a question paired with the answer. The next time you see the highlight, you'll be prompted with the question, forcing you to actively recall the answer. This is scientifically proven to form stronger memories enabling you to retain profoundly more of what you've read. To convert a highlight to Q&A, hit the Master icon in the web app (keyboard shortcut **M**).



## Question & Answer Tag



Not only can you convert a highlight to a Question & Answer flashcard while using Readwise, you can also create Q&A while you read. To create a flashcard while reading, simply highlight the passage containing the memorable idea and add a note beginning .qa (for question & answer). Then type your question to your future self inside of square brackets, ending with a question mark, followed by the answer in another set of brackets. When this highlight is resurfaced in Readwise, it will be in the form of a question. Talk about retaining what you read!
*Example Q&A note: .qa[What makes calico cats different from other colors of cat?][Calico cats are predominantly female.]*



## Themed Reviews



If you're trying to master a specific subject or working on a new project, you can create a [Themed Review](https://readwise.io/themed_reviews) and set it up to only display highlights with a specific tag, from a certain document, or any combination of the two. These reviews are like your Daily Review, only even more customizable. You can choose how many highlights you see per review, which days of the week and time of day the review refreshes, and if you want to receive the review as an email or only access it via the app.
Pro tip: Try tagging highlights that contain new or intriguing words, then set up a Themed Review for vocab with Q&A definitions. Next time you hear someone say, "oh dang, what was that word again," maybe you'll have the answer!



## Inline Tagging



Do you ever wish that your highlights were tagged with useful keywords and categories but never bothered because the process of tagging is just too cumbersome? Readwise offers an easy way to tag while reading call "inline tagging". An inline tag is a special note taken while you read that's automatically converted into a tag in Readwise. Tagging in the moment is much faster than tagging after the fact, and once your highlights have keywords and categories, they're much easier to review and reference. Simply highlight a passage and add a note beginning with a period (.) followed by a single word or abbreviation (with no spaces). You can also train Readwise to interpret shorthand! Read more here: [How to Tag Your Highlights While You Read](https://blog.readwise.io/tag-your-highlights-while-you-read/).



## Heading Tags (Creating a Table of Contents)



Did you know you can add chapters to your highlights in Readwise? In addition, you can generate a nifty table of contents which you can use to quickly navigate a book and refresh your memory of the book's organization.
To add this chapter data while reading, simply highlight the title of each section and add a note beginning with a period (.) followed by an h (for "heading") and then the number 1, 2, or 3 depending on the section's position in the hierarchy. For example, with a book organized into parts, chapters, and sections, you would denote all parts as .h1, all chapters as .h2, and all sections as .h3. Read more here: [How to Add Chapters to Your Highlights in Readwise](https://blog.readwise.io/add-chapters-to-highlights/).



## Concatenation Tags (Combining Highlights)



Have you ever found yourself highlighting an entire fluff-filled paragraph even though all you really wanted were the key sentences at the beginning and end? You can cut this fluff by taking special notes while you read. These notes instruct Readwise to combine multiple, non-adjacent highlights into a single annotation. Simply highlight the first string of text you want to combine and add the note .c1 ("c" for "concatenate"). Then, highlight the second string of text and add the note .c2. Upon importing into Readwise, these two highlights will be combined into a single annotation. Read more here: [How to Combine Highlights On-the-Fly with Readwise](https://blog.readwise.io/combine-highlights-on-the-fly/).



## Export to Note-Taking Apps



If you have a note-taking system in another app — such as Evernote, Notion, Obsidian, Roam, or a variety of others — you can use Readwise to automatically (and continuously) export all your highlights from all sources to your note-taking app. Simply select the [Export](https://readwise.io/export) option from the [Readwise Dashboard](https://readwise.io/dashboard), select your app of choice, and follow the instructions from there. *Note: Exporting highlights is a premium feature.*



## Deleting Books



There are two ways to handle books and articles you don't want to see in Readwise. The first method is to set the book's frequency to "Never" from the [Library](https://readwise.io/everything) view. The book will continue to be accessible from your library, but its highlights will not be resurfaced in your Daily Reviews. The second method is to delete the book so that it does not appear anywhere in Readwise. You can do this from the Library menu as well. Note that if you delete a book, Readwise will not re-import the book on subsequent resyncs.



## Export to Markdown



Many modern note-taking apps accept [Markdown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markdown) as their preferred import format of choice. Accordingly, Readwise enables you to export to Markdown all your notes and highlights on a book-by-basis for use elsewhere. Simply head to the [Export](https://readwise.io/export) page and select the [Markdown](https://readwise.io/export/markdown/preferences) option.



## Deleting Highlights in Kindle



We're often asked: What happens if I delete a highlight in Readwise? Does it disappear in Kindle? Conversely, we're also asked: What happens if I delete a highlight in Kindle? Does it disappear in Readwise? In both cases, the answer is no. If you delete a highlight in Kindle, it will be preserved in Readwise. If you delete a highlight in Readwise, it will be preserved in Kindle.



## Kindle Highlight Limits



If you're an avid highlighter of Kindle books, you've probably encountered the Kindle highlight limit. Basically, for copyright reasons, many publishers set limitations on how much of a book you can export through highlights. For example, if you highlight more than 10% of many books, those highlights above the limit will be truncated with a "...". We understand how incredibly frustrating this can be, since often the books we highlight the most are the ones we most want to post-process! Unfortunately, we have no way around this limit at this time.

## Highlights from PDFs



You can import a highlighted PDF to Readwise, and Readwise will find the highlighted passages and add them to your Readwise library. To do so, navigate to the [Export](https://readwise.io/export) page and select the [PDF](https://readwise.io/import/pdf/) option, then follow the instructions on the page.



## Streaks



As a little psychological nudge to help you build your daily review habit, we've built a streak counter. You maintain your streak by reviewing your daily highlights at least once per day (within the 24 hour window of your email send time). If you miss a few days due to vacation or illness or simply forgetting to do it, that's okay! You can save your streak by going to the [stats](https://readwise.io/stats) page and clicking on the days you need to recover.
