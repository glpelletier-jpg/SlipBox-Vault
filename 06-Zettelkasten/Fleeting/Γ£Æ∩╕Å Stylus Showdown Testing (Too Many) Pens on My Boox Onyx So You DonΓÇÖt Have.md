# **✒️ Stylus Showdown: Testing (Too Many) Pens on My Boox Onyx So You Don’t Have To**

## **I started with one pen. Now I have five. My wallet is mad, but my handwriting is happy.**

**Press enter or click to view image in full size**

[https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1050/1*FROUWhTrDtIe3LNpRM-CzQ.png](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1050/1*FROUWhTrDtIe3LNpRM-CzQ.png)

**It began innocently enough. My Boox Onyx Tab Ultra came with a pen. It worked. I used it. End of story, right?**

**Nope. Because one day I saw someone on Reddit mention the** **Staedtler Noris Digital** **and how it “feels like writing on paper.” Then someone else swore by the** **LAMY AL-star EMR** **for its “weighted balance.” And before I knew it, I was deep in the rabbit hole of EMR stylus comparisons, chasing the mythical perfect pen.**

**As a PhD student who uses Boox for everything — field notes, literature annotations, morning journaling, bad sketching when procrastinating — I thought I’d share what I’ve learned after testing** **five different styluses** **on my Boox Onyx device.**

**This is not a tech spec listicle. It’s a hands-on, slightly nerdy, slightly emotional reflection on what it’s like to live with these pens day-to-day in the academic trenches.**

## **My Setup**

**I tested all pens on a** **Boox Tab Ultra C** **(though the same applies to most Onyx Boox devices using** **Wacom EMR** **tech). No screen protector, just stock matte screen.**

**Styluses I tested:**

- **Boox Standard Pen (magnetic)** **— the one that comes in the box.**

- **Boox Pen 2 Pro** **— slightly upgraded, with an eraser and better grip.**

- **Staedtler Noris Digital** **— the “classic pencil” of EMR styluses.**

- **LAMY AL-star EMR** **— the fancy metal one that looks like a real pen.**

- **Samsung S Pen (from Tab S7+)** **— yes, it works too.**

**Let’s break it down.**

## **🖊️ Boox Standard Pen — Functional Minimalism**

**Let’s start with the default.**

**Pros:**

- **Light, simple, no buttons to accidentally press.**

- **Magnetic attachment works on newer Boox models.**

- **Pressure sensitivity is decent, and it glides smoothly.**

**Cons:**

- **Feels plasticky and toy-like.**

- **No eraser on the back.**

- **No grip; writing long notes makes your fingers slip over time.**

**Real-life feel:****It’s like the IKEA pencil of digital styluses — gets the job done, but don’t expect comfort or style. Fine for occasional use, but if you write a lot (like… 10,000 thesis-related words a week), it starts to feel tiring.**

**Would I keep using it?****Only if I lost the others.**

## **✨ Boox Pen 2 Pro — The Actually-Good Upgrade**

**This is Boox’s premium pen. It looks… very similar to the default. But it feels like someone finally thought about what writers actually need.**

**Pros:**

- **Rubberized grip = more control, less fatigue.**

- **Built-in** **eraser on the back** **(love this).**

- **Magnetic, same pressure curve as the basic pen.**

**Cons:**

- **Still very light — some may prefer more heft.**

- **Slightly overpriced for what’s essentially a quality-of-life boost.**

**Real-life feel:****This is the pen I reach for when I want smooth writing and quick corrections. The eraser button alone justifies the upgrade if you annotate PDFs a lot. It’s not sexy, but it’s reliable.**

**Would I keep using it?****Yes. It’s the one I carry daily in my backpack.**

## **✏️ Staedtler Noris Digital — Writing Nostalgia Unlocked**

**Now** ***this*** **is the one everyone romanticizes. And I get it.**

**Pros:**

- **Looks and feels like a real pencil — wooden texture, hexagonal body.**

- **Incredibly precise tip, slightly more friction = paper-like feel.**

- **Lightweight but balanced.**

- **Built-in eraser.**

**Cons:**

- **No magnet.**

- **Can feel a bit short if you’re used to longer pens.**

**Real-life feel:****It** ***feels*** **like you’re writing in a notebook again. This is my go-to for brainstorming, mind mapping, or when I want to think by hand. The tactile feel is unmatched.**

**Would I keep using it?****Absolutely — for slow, thoughtful writing sessions. It’s my “sit in a café and look wise” pen.**

## **🖋️ LAMY AL-star EMR — The Executive Choice**

**This is the stylus that whispers, “You have read Heidegger in the original German.”**

**Pros:**

- **Beautiful metal body, classic LAMY design.**

- **Refillable nibs, robust build.**

- ***Heft*** **— it feels like a fountain pen.**

**Cons:**

- **The grip is polarizing — LAMY uses a shaped triangle grip.**

- **The weight makes it a bit tiring for long writing sessions.**

- **No eraser.**

**Real-life feel:****It’s luxurious. The pen glides, and the balance feels deliberate. But it demands proper posture and a slow pace. Not ideal for quick scribbling. More of a stylus for deliberate annotation or when you want to feel like a scholar rather than a note goblin.**

**Would I keep using it?****Yes, but selectively — this is the one I use when writing a letter to future me or annotating important texts with extra reverence.**

## **🧩 Samsung S Pen (Tablet Edition) — Surprisingly Compatible**

**I didn’t expect much, but the S Pen from my old Galaxy Tab works great.**

**Pros:**

- **Small, portable, pressure sensitivity works fine.**

- **Has a programmable button.**

- **Handy if you already own a Samsung tablet.**

**Cons:**

- **No eraser, no grip.**

- **Slight latency hiccups compared to Boox pens.**

- **Feels too small for serious writing.**

**Real-life feel:****This is my emergency pen. I keep it in a pouch as a backup. It works, but feels like writing with a stick of gum.**

**Would I keep using it?****Only if everything else fails.**

## **Final Thoughts from a Pen-Hoarding PhD Student**

**So, which one is** ***the best*****?**

**Honestly… it depends.**

- **For** **everyday research use****, the** **Boox Pen 2 Pro** **is the most balanced.**

- **For** **long reflective writing****, the** **Staedtler** **makes it feel like paper again.**

- **For** **aesthetic power moves****, the** **LAMY** **is unbeatable (especially in public).**

- **And the** **Standard Pen****? It’s fine. Just… fine.**

**I never meant to collect styluses, but each one has its moment. And there’s something oddly comforting about choosing a pen to match your writing mood — kind of like choosing the right mug for your tea.**

**Because in the end, writing — digital or not — is still deeply personal.**

**✍️**

**Let me know what pen you swear by, or if I missed one worth trying (my bank account says no, but my curiosity says yes).**
