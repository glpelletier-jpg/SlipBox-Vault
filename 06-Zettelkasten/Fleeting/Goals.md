## Goals

There are two main types of Goals:

1. Value Goals: are more meaningful and relevant. They're not measurable and highlight what really is important to you.

2. Goal Outcomes: these move us towards a specific measurable outcome

## Fall in love with Systems

- None of this is to say that goals are useless. Goals are good for *planning* your progress and systems are good for actually *making* progress.

- Goals can provide direction and even push you forward in the short-term, but eventually a well-designed system will always win. Having a system is what matters. Committing to the process is what makes the difference.

- *The fastest way to make progress on your* ***Goals*** *is to* ***FOCUS*** *on one goal at a time*

>- **Doing more things doesn't drive faster or better results**. >- **Doing better things drives better results**. > - **Even more accurately, doing one thing as best you can drives better results.** > > - *Your odds of success improve when you are forced to direct all of your energy and attention to fewer tasks.* > > - *Research has shown that you are 2x to 3x more likely to stick to your goals if you make a specific plan for when, where, and how you will perform the behaviour.*
