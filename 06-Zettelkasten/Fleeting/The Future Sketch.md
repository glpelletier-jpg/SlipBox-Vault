## Introduction to the Future Sketch

Create a compelling vision that represents your future 3 years from now in line with your North Star. By identifying your direction, you’ll be able to take more effective steps towards reaching your milestones.

## 🥜 In a Nutshell

Future Sketch has four key components:

- **Odyssey Plan**: Exploring different life directions to avoid getting stuck

- **3-Year Dream**: Visualising your ideal life 3 years from now

- **Vision Board**: Creating a visual representation of your dream life

- **Future Calendar**: Mapping out your ideal days 3 years ahead

👉 Result: Clarity on where you're heading and what to prioritise now

Your Future Sketch is where your long-term vision comes to closer view. It’s about imagining, in vivid detail, what your life could look like three years from now.

> For your dreams to greatly influence you and the future to pull you, your future must be well planned. There are two ways to face the future—one is with apprehension, the other with anticipation. Many people face the future with apprehension. Why? They donʼt have it well designed; and without really thinking about it, they have probably bought someone elseʼs view of how to live. - Jim Rohn, *The Art of Exceptional Living*

We’re trying to create our own way to live. We’re trying to create our own map for like, roughly where do I think I wanna be going in the course of my life?

❓**But life doesn’t happen according to plan!**

There’s all these quotes like, “No plan survives the first contact with the enemy” and “Everyone’s got a plan until they get punched in the face”. Anything could happen. We never know when life is going to punch us in the face, but at least until it does, we can have a reasonable plan.



## **✍🏻 Why plan for the next 3 years?**

It’s very difficult to visualise 10 years out because who knows what the world will be like and what our lives are going to look like? But you can probably imagine 3 years from now, what could change about your life, what might change about your life. It’s a bit easier to visualise than 5 years and 10 years. 3 years is the upper limit of how far most people can feasibly foresee their life before stuff really starts to change.

- A 3 year time horizon offers room for growth while remaining realistic

- Bridges the gap between your life compass (long-term vision) and quarterly quests (short-term goals)

- A clear destination keeps you inspired and focused

- Knowing what matters most to you in the medium term offers clarity on what requires prioritisation now

There are several exercises that can help us visualise what we want our life to look like three years from now. We’ll walk through them below.



# Your Odyssey Plan

## ⚓ The Odyssey Plan

I first did this exercise in December 2019, and it was my first serious attempt at a life visualization and design exercise. I discovered it through a YouTube video from a friend who referenced the book Designing Your Life by Bill Burnett and Dave Evans, Stanford Business School professors who teach a course on applying design thinking principles to life planning. This exercise asks you to imagine three distinct versions of your life five years from now.



[💡Exercise: The Odyssey Plan](../Tasks/%F0%9F%92%A1Exercise%20The%20Odyssey%20Plan.md)


### **Example: Ali’s Odyssey Plan from 2019**

I did this exercise five years ago and wrote about it in my newsletter, which is still on my website.

🎯 **Current Path:** At the time, I was 25 and visualising my life at age 30. When I wrote out my default path, I envisioned myself training in anaesthesiology at Cambridge, working part-time as a doctor because I enjoyed it, continuing with YouTube, still being on the dating scene because I hadn't found "the perfect person" (and was overthinking it), and teaching medical physiology part-time at Cambridge University, which I thought would be really fun.

When I saw all of that written out, I had this profound feeling of "ugh, is that it?" This was the life I had been working towards, a life many people would dream of. I mean, being a lecturer at Cambridge University, working as an anaesthetics trainee, saving lives… it looked amazing on paper. But seeing it written out, it just felt kind of boring. It no longer inspired me. While it had once been exciting, I realised this path was no longer energising. That was really the beginning of the end of my medical career - doing this exercise and realising "ugh, this sounds a bit grim."

🐉 **Alternative Path:** My alternative path showed my lack of imagination. I basically just thought "well, instead of doing medicine in the UK, I'll do medicine in the US." I planned to take the USMLE (United States Medical Licensing Examination) and become a resident there, maybe train in surgery. I even started preparing for the USMLE in January, five years ago, seriously considering moving to America.

🦄 **Radical Path:** But when I got to the radical path - imagining what I'd do if I didn't need to worry about money or what people would think - I questioned whether I'd even do medicine at all. I realized I probably wouldn't. I'd probably do some sort of startup or become a full-time YouTuber. And interestingly, that radical path ended up coming much closer to reality than I could have imagined at the time.



### 📚 Resource recommendations to dive deeper

# Your Three Year Dream

## ☁️ The 3-Year Dream

Imagine yourself walking up a mountain and entering a huge cavern, a really, really big cave. As you walk inside, you see **three mirrors🪞**.

Going up to the **first mirror**, you see yourself - but not just as you are now. You see yourself three years older, living your absolute dream life in the domain of yourself. You're in perfect shape, whatever that means for you physically and mentally. In the mirror, you see yourself doing a hobby that brings you loads of joy. You can sense, looking at yourself, that you've grown in so many ways over these three years. This is the mirror of your dream life in the domain of self: your health, hobbies, and personal growth.

Then you move to the **second mirror**. You're also three years older, and just from how you're standing and what you're wearing, you can tell you're having a really fulfilled time at work. You're doing that thing you've always wanted to do, and you've made it happen. You're so proud of yourself for making that happen and for having the impact in the world that you dreamed of. You're making the money you want to make, working with people you love, doing work you love.

Finally, you approach the **third mirror**. Like the Mirror of Erised in *Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone*, this mirror shows you three years from now, surrounded with your friends and family, all your most important relationships. You can feel yourself being fulfilled with the glow of these happy relationships all around you.

# Your Vision Board

## 🖼️ Vision Board

**A vision board is a powerful pictorial representation that displays your goals**, dreams, and aspirations, serving as a daily reminder of what you're working toward.

Your vision board could be analog (hung by your bedside, for example) or digital (set as your lock screen). The important thing is to keep it in sight.

### **🔬 The Science on Why This Works: Reticular Activating System (RAS)**

**Our brains are constantly bombarded with sensory inputs**, from what we see and hear to our physical sensations and thoughts. There's so much information pouring in that if we actually processed everything consciously, we would literally go insane from information overload. Fortunately, we've evolved a protective mechanism called selective attention, which is mediated by the reticular activating system (RAS), a part near the base of the brain dedicated to filtering information and deciding what's important.

**The RAS categorises about 99% of incoming information as "not important,"** letting it stay in our subconscious and pass by unnoticed. But that crucial 1% that gets flagged as important makes it into our conscious awareness. As humans, we're particularly visual creatures. We have an entire lobe of the brain (the occipital lobe) dedicated just to processing visual information, which shows how important visual processing is to our brains.

**This is why looking at a vision board daily can be so powerful**. It helps tap into this visual processing system and aligns your head and heart, signalling to your RAS that these images and goals are important. When you do this, information that your RAS might have previously filtered out as irrelevant suddenly becomes conscious and actionable. You start noticing opportunities related to your dreams and vision that you might have missed before, potentially leading to life-changing opportunities.

# Your Future Calendar

## 📆 Future Calendar

Now that we've looked at what your dream life looks like in terms of self, work, and relationships, let's project forward three years and examine what your daily schedule might look like.

Imagine an ideal Tuesday three years from now. maybe you've quit your job and become an entrepreneur, or maybe you've got kids by then, which would completely change how your ideal day looks.

Let’s also look at your ideal Sunday. We tend to fixate on work stuff when it comes to productivity (hence focusing on Tuesday, a workday), but it's just as important to envision how you want to spend your time when you're not working.
