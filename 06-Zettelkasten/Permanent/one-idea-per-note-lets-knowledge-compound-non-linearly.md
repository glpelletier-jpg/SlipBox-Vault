# One idea per note lets knowledge compound non-linearly

> **One idea. One note.** Write for your future self — complete enough to understand without context.

---

## The idea

A note that contains only one idea can be reused in any context where that idea is relevant. A note that contains five ideas can only be reused when all five ideas are relevant — which is almost never. Atomicity is what makes a note a building block rather than a document.

This is structurally identical to the software principle of *separation of concerns*: a module that does one thing is far more composable than one that does many. The moment you want to reuse just part of a multi-concern note, you can't — you either copy it (creating drift) or leave the connection unmade (wasting the insight).

The compounding effect is non-linear: each new atomic note can form a connection with every existing note. Ten atomic notes create up to 45 possible connections. Ten topic-dump notes create almost none, because the signal is buried.

---

## Why it matters

This is the primary reason most people's note systems feel like archives rather than thinking tools. They accumulate notes (information storage) but not ideas (composable units). The vault grows but the thinking doesn't.

The atomicity principle is what separates a slip box from a folder of saved articles.

---

## Evidence / examples

Andy Matuschak: *"It's easier to form connections across topics and contexts. If your notes are too broad, you might not notice when you encounter some new idea about one of the notions contained within."*

Zettelkasten principle of atomicity from zettelkasten.de: *"Atomicity fosters re-use which in turn multiplies the amount of connections in the network of Zettels."*
