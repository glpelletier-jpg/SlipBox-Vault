# HAM Radio MOC

> Navigation map for amateur radio knowledge — from exam prep through operating practice. Anchored to the General class license goal in [[01-Vision/future-vision|Future Vision]].

---

## What this map covers

The theory, regulations, operating practice, and equipment knowledge for amateur radio — organized to track from exam prep through active operating. Bridges to [[06-Zettelkasten/MOC/Automation Engineering MOC|Automation Engineering MOC]] on electrical fundamentals, which appear in both the CAP and General exam pools.

---

## License progression

```
Technician ──→ General ──→ Amateur Extra
(HF limited)   (Full HF)   (All privileges)
                  ↑
            Current goal
```

- **Prerequisite:** Must hold Technician class license before sitting General exam
- **General exam:** 35 questions, 26/35 to pass, pool valid July 2023 – June 2027

### Radio Wave Propagation

*G3 subelement — 3 of 35 exam questions*

Key topics: ionospheric layers (D, E, F1, F2), sky wave vs. ground wave, solar cycle effects, gray line propagation, multipath, Doppler shift.

This is a conceptually rich area — propagation is why certain bands work at certain times of day and year. Understanding it makes operating intuitive rather than trial-and-error.

- [[05-Resources/Courses/HAM-Radio/General License Syllabus|Syllabus — Week 3 (G3): Radio Wave Propagation]]
- [[06-Zettelkasten/Literature/The Fast Track to Understanding Ham Radio Propagation|Ham Radio Propagation (literature note)]]
- [[06-Zettelkasten/Literature/The ARRL Handbook for Radio Communications 100th Edition; Volume 1|ARRL Handbook Vol 1]]

### Antennas & Feed Lines

*G9 subelement — 4 of 35 exam questions*

Key topics: dipole and Yagi antenna characteristics, SWR and impedance matching, coax feed lines, baluns, antenna tuners, gain vs. directivity.

SWR math overlaps with the [[#electrical-principles]] cluster — same impedance concepts, applied to RF.

### Station Equipment & Assembly

*G4 + G7 subelements — ~4 of 35 exam questions*

Key topics: transceiver architecture, receiver sensitivity and selectivity, transmitter stages, power supplies, SWR meters, dummy loads, filters, PTT/VOX.

- [[05-Resources/Courses/HAM-Radio/General License Syllabus|Syllabus — Weeks 4 & 7 (G4, G7): Equipment]]

### Digital Modes

*G8 subelement — 4 of 35 exam questions*

Key topics: FT8, WSPR, PSK31, JS8Call, APRS, packet radio, bandwidth and baud rate rules, HF digital operating frequencies.

Digital modes are where HAM radio intersects most directly with IT/networking skills.

- [[05-Resources/Courses/HAM-Radio/Ham radio digital modes|Ham radio digital modes]]
- [[05-Resources/Courses/HAM-Radio/General License Syllabus|Syllabus — Week 8 (G8): Signals & Emissions]]

---

### Safety

*G0 subelement — 2 of 35 exam questions*

Key topics: RF exposure limits, MPE (Maximum Permissible Exposure), station grounding, lightning protection, tower safety.

RF safety math (power density calculations) is straightforward if you're comfortable with power formulas from [[#electrical-principles]].

- [[05-Resources/Courses/HAM-Radio/General License Syllabus|Syllabus — Week 10 (G0): Safety]]
