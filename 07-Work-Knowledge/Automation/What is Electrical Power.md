# What is Electrical Power?

[When work is done at a certain speed, that influences the power of the system](https://control.com/uploads/thumbnails/Ch_4.2_DC_Theory_.jpg)

### When work is done at a certain speed, that influences the power of the system

Electrical power is the speed (technically, the rate) at which energy is produced or consumed. Electrical power is increased by raising the current or voltage, thereby lowering the time in which the energy conversion takes place.

Since we often use electricity to convey energy from one location to another, it is important to be able to quantify and calculate the rate at which energy is delivered by an electric circuit. The rate at which energy transfers from one location to another is technically referred to as power (), and it is typically measured in the unit of the *watt* (1 watt = 1 joule of energy per second of time).

### Watt’s Law

The total amount of power (energy per unit time) carried by an electric current is proportional to the strength of that current as well as the amount of potential (voltage) between the poles of the electrical source.
