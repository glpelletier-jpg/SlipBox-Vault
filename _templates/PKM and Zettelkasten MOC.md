# PKM and Zettelkasten MOC

> Navigation note for personal knowledge management — Zettelkasten methodology, note-writing practice, progressive summarization, and the theory of how knowledge compounds. The largest cluster in the Fleeting inbox: 45+ notes ready to seed permanent notes.

---

## What this map covers

How to capture, process, and connect ideas so that knowledge compounds over time instead of decaying in a collection. Grounded in Niklas Luhmann's Zettelkasten method, extended by Andy Matuschak's evergreen note work, and applied through this vault.

## Core tensions in this theme

- **Collection vs. processing** — most note-takers collect obsessively but process rarely; the value is in the processing
- **Atomic vs. comprehensive** — one idea per note vs. trying to summarize everything in one place
- **Tags vs. links** — retrieval by category vs. retrieval by connection; links win for insight
- **Structure first vs. emergence** — pre-designed folder hierarchies vs. letting structure emerge from the notes themselves
