## Ham radio

Ham radio network types vary by frequency (**HF for long-distance, VHF/UHF for local/FM), operating mode (Voice, Morse Code, Digital data like Packet/APRS), and structure, including Nets (ARES/RACES for emergency, Club Nets, Traffic Nets), Repeaters, and Satellite communication**, all connecting operators for self-training, emergency comms, and experimentation. [[1](https://outdoorempire.com/ham-radio-101/), [2](https://emcomminfo.com/ham-radio-nets/), [3](http://www.arrl.org/modes-systems#:~:text=Ham%20radio%20modes%20can%20be%20modulation%20modes,and%20special%20systems%20on%20the%20ARRL%20web.), [4](https://www.fcc.gov/wireless/bureau-divisions/mobility-division/amateur-radio-service)]

By Frequency & Range

- **HF (High Frequency) (Shortwave):** Long-distance (DX) over the horizon via ionospheric reflection; great for global contacts, especially at night (e.g., 80m, 40m).

- **VHF (Very High Frequency) & UHF (Ultra High Frequency):** Mostly "line-of-sight," great for local/regional comms, FM voice, and repeaters (e.g., 2m & 70cm bands).

- **LF (Low Frequency)/MF (Medium Frequency):** Used for specialized long-distance data/voice on bands like 2200m and 160m. [[5](https://tigrettod.com/blogs/tigrett-outdoors-blog/ham-radio-range), [6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amateur_radio_frequency_allocations), [7](https://strykerradios.com/ham-radios/ham-radio-frequencies-common-uses-you-should-know/), [8](https://www.hamradioschool.com/post/what-are-the-different-kinds-of-ham-radios), [9](https://efom.crs.org/operational-setup/office-set-up-introduction/voice-communications/radio/#:~:text=Two%20general%20types%20of%20radio%20communications%20are,Frequency%20(VHF)%20(or%20Ultra%20High%20Frequency%20(UHF).)]

By Network Structure & Purpose

- **Nets (Network Operations):** Scheduled or spontaneous gatherings for specific purposes.

    - **Emergency Nets:** ARES (Amateur Radio Emergency Service) & RACES for public service/disaster relief.

    - **Traffic Nets:** Relay messages (traffic) across areas.

    - **Club/Topic Nets:** For club business or discussing hobbies.

    - **DX Nets:** Focus on contacting distant stations.

- **Repeaters:** VHF/UHF stations that receive weak signals and retransmit them from a higher location, extending range significantly.

- **Digital Networks:** Use computers for data (Packet, APRS, Hamnet/IP) for messaging and data sharing, sometimes linking to the internet where permitted.

- **Satellite Comms:** Communicating through amateur radio satellites. [[2](https://emcomminfo.com/ham-radio-nets/), [3](http://www.arrl.org/modes-systems#:~:text=Ham%20radio%20modes%20can%20be%20modulation%20modes,and%20special%20systems%20on%20the%20ARRL%20web.), [6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amateur_radio_frequency_allocations), [10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_to4_BWhE_4#:~:text=A%20wide%20range%20of%20frequencies%20are%20available,their%20favorite%20listening%20frequencies%20in%20the%20comments.), [11](https://www.ardc.net/wp-content/uploads/brieflook.pdf), [12](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amateur_radio_net#:~:text=Types%20of%20nets%20*%20Traffic.%20*%20DX.%20*%20Club%20or%20Topic.)]

By Operating Mode (How you talk)

- **Voice:** FM (local), SSB (long-distance), AM (vintage/niche).

- **Morse Code (CW):** Weak-signal, simple, and efficient.

- **Digital:** Packet, PSK31, FT8, RTTY (computer-based data).

- **Image:** SSTV (Slow Scan TV), ATV (Amateur Television). [[3](http://www.arrl.org/modes-systems#:~:text=Ham%20radio%20modes%20can%20be%20modulation%20modes,and%20special%20systems%20on%20the%20ARRL%20web.), [11](https://www.ardc.net/wp-content/uploads/brieflook.pdf), [13](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amateur_radio)]
