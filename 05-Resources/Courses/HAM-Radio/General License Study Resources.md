# HAM Radio General License — Study Resources

> General class is the second license level after Technician. It unlocks HF (high-frequency) privileges — the bands used for long-distance and international communication.
> Current question pool: **2023–2027** (valid through June 30, 2027). 35 questions, need 26 correct (74%) to pass.

---

## Exam overview

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Questions on exam | 35 multiple choice |
| Passing score | 26/35 (74%) |
| Question pool size | ~429 questions |
| Pool valid | July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2027 |
| Prerequisites | Must hold Technician license |
| Privileges gained | Full HF access (160m–10m), additional VHF/UHF |

---

## Subelements (what's on the test)

| Code | Topic | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| G0 | Electrical & RF Safety | Overlaps with electrical fundamentals in [[07-Work-Knowledge/Automation/Automation|Automation folder]] |
| G1 | FCC Rules & Regulations | License classes, frequency privileges, station ID |
| G2 | Operating Procedures | HF operating, nets, digital modes, emergency ops |
| G3 | Radio Wave Propagation | Ionosphere, solar cycles, band conditions |
| G4 | Amateur Radio Practices | Station setup, test equipment, interference |
| G5 | Electrical Principles | Ohm's Law, impedance, resonance, decibels |
| G6 | Circuit Components | Transistors, diodes, op-amps, filters |
| G7 | Practical Circuits | Power supplies, amplifiers, receivers |
| G8 | Signals & Emissions | Modulation types, bandwidth, digital modes |
| G9 | Antennas & Transmission Lines | Dipoles, verticals, SWR, feed lines, connectors |

> **Note:** G0 and G5 overlap heavily with electrical principles in `07-Work-Knowledge/Automation/`. Study those together.

## Suggested study sequence

1. **Read** one subelement of the ARRL manual (or KB6NU guide)
2. **Drill** that subelement on HamStudy.org until >85% accuracy
3. **Take** a full 35-question practice exam on ARRL Exam Review
4. **Repeat** — move to next subelement
5. When consistently scoring 30+/35 on practice exams, schedule the exam at [arrl.org/find-an-amateur-radio-license-exam-session](https://www.arrl.org/find-an-amateur-radio-license-exam-session)

**Time estimate:** 30–40 hours of study. At 30 min/day, roughly 8–10 weeks.
