## Week 1 — Commission's Rules (G1)

**Subelement G1 · 54 questions in pool · 5 questions on exam**

The regulatory foundation. FCC rules, license privileges, frequency allocations, station identification.

### Topics
- **G1A** — General, Amateur, and Technician control operator privileges on HF
- **G1B** — Antenna height, beacon stations, prohibited transmissions, emergency rules
- **G1C** — Transmitter power regulations, data emission standards, 60-meter band
- **G1D** — Volunteer examiners, temporary operating permits, reciprocal licenses
- **G1E** — Control categories, repeater regulations, harmful interference, third-party rules

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | Read G1A + G1B — study on HamStudy.org |
| Tue | Read G1C + G1D — drill flashcards |
| Wed | Read G1E — complete subelement quiz |
| Thu | HamStudy G1 drill until >80% accuracy |
| Fri | Full 35-question practice exam (baseline score) |

### Key things to know
- General class frequency privileges by band (memorize the HF table)
- Power limits: 1500W PEP on most HF, 200W on 60m
- Station ID requirements: every 10 minutes and at end of transmission

## Week 2 — Operating Procedures (G2)

**Subelement G2 · 60 questions in pool · 5 questions on exam**

How to actually operate on HF — making contacts, nets, digital modes, emergency procedures.

### Topics
- **G2A** — Phone operating procedures, USB/LSB conventions, calling frequencies
- **G2B** — Operating courtesy, band plans, DX operating
- **G2C** — CW operating procedures, prosigns, Q-signals
- **G2D** — Amateur auxiliary, HF operations, Winlink, digital operating
- **G2E** — Digital operating, RTTY/data emissions, PSK, FT8/FT4

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G2A + G2B — phone and DX procedures |
| Tue | G2C — CW prosigns and Q-signals (no sending required, just recognition) |
| Wed | G2D + G2E — digital modes (connects to [[Ham radio digital modes|digital modes notes]]) |
| Thu | HamStudy G2 drill until >80% |
| Fri | Practice exam + review week 1–2 weak areas |

### Key things to know
- USB above 10 MHz, LSB below 10 MHz (with exceptions)
- Q-signals: QRM, QRN, QSB, QRP, QRZ, QSL, QSY, QTH
- FT8 uses 15-second transmission cycles on specific frequencies

---

## Week 3 — Radio Wave Propagation (G3)

**Subelement G3 · 37 questions in pool · 3 questions on exam**

How radio waves travel — ionosphere layers, solar effects, band characteristics by time of day and season.

### Topics
- **G3A** — Sunspot cycles, solar flux index, geomagnetic disturbances, propagation forecasts
- **G3B** — MUF (Maximum Usable Frequency), LUF, skip zone, ground wave vs. sky wave
- **G3C** — Ionospheric layers (D, E, F1, F2), critical frequency, sporadic-E

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G3A — solar cycles and their effect on HF |
| Tue | G3B — MUF, skip, and path prediction |
| Wed | G3C — ionospheric layers and their behavior |
| Thu | HamStudy G3 drill + review G1 and G2 |
| Fri | Practice exam |

### Key things to know
- F2 layer: highest, most important for long-distance HF (especially 10–20m)
- D layer: absorbs signals, strongest during day, disappears at night (why 40m opens up at night)
- Higher sunspot numbers = better high-band (10m, 15m) propagation
- MUF: highest frequency that will return to Earth via ionosphere on a given path

## Week 4 — Amateur Radio Practices (G4)

**Subelement G4 · 60 questions in pool · 5 questions on exam**

Practical station operation — test equipment, interference, transceiver operation, digital setup.

### Topics
- **G4A** — Station operation, transceiver controls, ALC, RF gain, noise blanker
- **G4B** — Test and monitoring equipment, oscilloscope, SWR/wattmeter, signal tracer
- **G4C** — Interference to consumer electronics, bonding, filtering RFI
- **G4D** — Speech processors, S-meters, SSB/CW signal monitoring
- **G4E** — HF mobile operation, solar power, antenna alternatives

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G4A — transceiver controls and operating adjustments |
| Tue | G4B — test equipment (oscilloscope, SWR meter, dummy load) |
| Wed | G4C + G4D — interference and monitoring |
| Thu | G4E — mobile and alternative power |
| Fri | HamStudy G4 drill + full practice exam |

### Key things to know
- ALC (Automatic Level Control): prevents overdriving the final amplifier
- SWR of 1:1 is perfect match; above 3:1 may damage transmitter
- Use a dummy load when testing transmitters off-air

## Week 5 — Electrical Principles (G5)

**Subelement G5 · 40 questions in pool · 3 questions on exam**

> ⚡ **Overlap alert:** This subelement covers Ohm's Law, impedance, resonance, and power calculations — all topics also in [[07-Work-Knowledge/Automation/Automation|Work Knowledge / Automation]] and on the CAP exam. Study these together.

### Topics
- **G5A** — Impedance, reactance, resistance in AC circuits
- **G5B** — Power calculations, decibels, RMS voltage, PEP
- **G5C** — Series/parallel circuits, transformers, capacitors in circuits

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G5A — reactance, impedance, phase angle |
| Tue | G5B — power, decibels (memorize dB rules: +3dB = 2×, +10dB = 10×) |
| Wed | G5C — series/parallel circuits and transformers |
| Thu | HamStudy G5 drill |
| Fri | Practice exam + update Work Knowledge notes with overlapping concepts |

### Key things to know
- Impedance = √(R² + X²)
- PEP (Peak Envelope Power): what matters for SSB transmitters
- +3dB doubles power; −3dB halves it
- Resonant frequency: f = 1/(2π√LC)

## Week 6 — Circuit Components (G6)

**Subelement G6 · 23 questions in pool · 2 questions on exam**

Electronic components — semiconductors, op-amps, filters, digital logic.

### Topics
- **G6A** — Resistors, capacitors, inductors, rectifier diodes, zener diodes, LEDs
- **G6B** — Transistors (BJT, FET, MOSFET), op-amps, quartz crystals, MMICs
- **G6C** — Analog and digital ICs, logic gates, display types

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G6A — passive components |
| Tue | G6B — transistors and op-amps |
| Wed | G6C — ICs and logic gates |
| Thu | HamStudy G6 drill (lightest week — use extra time to review G3–G5) |
| Fri | Practice exam |

### Key things to know
- MOSFET: voltage-controlled, high input impedance
- Zener diode: used for voltage regulation (operates in reverse breakdown)
- AND, OR, NAND, NOR, XOR gate truth tables

## Week 7 — Practical Circuits (G7)

**Subelement G7 · 38 questions in pool · 3 questions on exam**

How circuits work together — power supplies, amplifiers, receivers, digital systems.

### Topics
- **G7A** — Power supply circuits: rectifiers, filters, regulators, voltages
- **G7B** — Amplifiers: classes A/B/C/D, push-pull, gain, audio amplifiers
- **G7C** — Receivers and transceivers: superheterodyne, IF, mixers, detectors, AGC

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G7A — power supplies and rectification |
| Tue | G7B — amplifier classes and operation |
| Wed | G7C — receivers and transceivers |
| Thu | HamStudy G7 drill |
| Fri | Practice exam — target: consistently 28+/35 by now |

### Key things to know
- Class C amplifier: most efficient but most distortion — used for FM, not SSB
- Class A: least efficient, lowest distortion — used for linear amplification
- Superheterodyne receiver converts incoming signal to fixed IF for easier filtering

---

## Week 8 — Signals & Emissions (G8)

**Subelement G8 · 42 questions in pool · 3 questions on exam**

Modulation types, bandwidth, digital emissions, spread spectrum.

### Topics
- **G8A** — Carrier and modulation: AM, FM, SSB, PM, DSB; modulation index
- **G8B** — Frequency mixing, intermodulation, 3rd order intercept, spurious emissions
- **G8C** — Digital emission modes: AFSK, FSK, PSK31, packet, APRS, spread spectrum

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G8A — modulation types and bandwidth |
| Tue | G8B — mixing, intermodulation, spurious signals |
| Wed | G8C — digital modes (connects to G2E notes) |
| Thu | HamStudy G8 drill |
| Fri | Practice exam |

### Key things to know
- SSB bandwidth: ~2.7 kHz · AM: ~6 kHz · FM (narrow): ~16 kHz
- APRS: Automatic Position Reporting System — GPS position via packet radio
- Intermodulation distortion (IMD): unwanted signals from two or more signals mixing

## Week 9 — Antennas & Feed Lines (G9)

**Subelement G9 · 46 questions in pool · 4 questions on exam**

The heaviest single-topic section. Antenna design, impedance matching, SWR, feed lines.

### Topics
- **G9A** — Feed lines: coax, ladder line, SWR, characteristic impedance, velocity factor
- **G9B** — Basic antennas: dipole, vertical, radiation patterns, takeoff angle
- **G9C** — Directional antennas: Yagi, quad, driven element, reflector, director, gain
- **G9D** — Specialized antennas: mobile, portable, multi-band, antenna tuners, NVIS

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G9A — feed lines, SWR, coax losses |
| Tue | G9B — dipoles and verticals, radiation patterns |
| Wed | G9C — Yagi and directional antennas |
| Thu | G9D — specialty antennas and tuners |
| Fri | HamStudy G9 drill + full practice exam |

### Key things to know
- Half-wave dipole length (ft): 468 / f(MHz)
- Quarter-wave vertical length (ft): 234 / f(MHz)
- Yagi gain: more elements = more gain = narrower beamwidth
- NVIS (Near Vertical Incidence Skywave): for regional HF comm, 0–300 miles

## Week 10 — Electrical & RF Safety (G0) + Final Review

**Subelement G0 · 25 questions in pool · 2 questions on exam**

RF exposure limits, safety practices, grounding, lightning protection.

### Topics
- **G0A** — RF safety: MPE limits, duty cycle, controlled vs. uncontrolled environments
- **G0B** — Station safety: grounding, lightning protection, fuses, interlock, tower climbing

### Daily plan
| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| Mon | G0A — RF exposure and MPE limits |
| Tue | G0B — station safety, grounding, lightning protection |
| Wed | Full review — HamStudy.org full test mode, 3 complete exams |
| Thu | Drill all subelements below 85% accuracy on HamStudy |
| Fri | Final practice exam — if scoring 30+/35 consistently, schedule the real exam |

### Key things to know
- MPE: Maximum Permissible Exposure — FCC limits based on frequency and power
- Controlled environment (you and informed bystanders) has higher limits than uncontrolled
- Always use a fuse/breaker rated for the wire, not the load
