## The exam at a glance

- **35 questions** drawn from a pool of ~425
- **Pass score:** 26/35 (74%)
- **Pool valid:** July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2027
- **Prerequisite:** Must hold Technician class license
- **What you gain:** Full HF privileges (160m–10m), additional VHF/UHF bands, higher power limits

## Primary study tools — how to use each

### 1. HamStudy.org (free) ← start here
**URL:** [hamstudy.org/general2023](https://hamstudy.org/general2023)

The single most useful study tool. Create a free account (tracks your progress across sessions).

**How to use it:**
- Study mode: shows flashcards one subelement at a time. Go subelement by subelement matching the syllabus weeks.
- Drill mode: keeps showing you questions you get wrong until you get them right.
- Test mode: generates a full 35-question practice exam from the real pool. Use this weekly to track overall progress.
- Progress dashboard: shows your accuracy per subelement as a percentage. Target >85% on every subelement before scheduling the real exam.

**Key insight:** HamStudy's algorithm knows which questions you struggle with and surfaces them more often. Trust the system — don't skip to test mode until it shows you're ready.

---

### 2. KB6NU No-Nonsense General Class Guide (free PDF)
**URL:** [kb6nu.com/study-guides](https://www.kb6nu.com/study-guides/)

A free, dense, well-written PDF covering the entire General question pool in plain English. Written by Dan Romanchik (KB6NU) — an experienced ham and educator.

**How to use it:**
- Download the PDF for the 2023–2027 pool.
- Read one subelement per week alongside HamStudy drills. It explains *why* each answer is correct, not just what the answer is. Understanding why makes the knowledge stick longer and helps you on questions you haven't seen before.
- Use it as a reference during study (Ctrl+F for any topic).
- It's also available as a printed book on Amazon if you prefer physical study materials.

**Why it's valuable:** The ARRL manual is organized by topic depth (great for learning); the KB6NU guide is organized by question pool (great for exam prep). Use both if you have time; use KB6NU alone if you want the most efficient path.

---

### 3. ARRL General Class License Manual, 10th Edition
**URL:** [arrl.org/general-class-license-manual](http://www.arrl.org/general-class-license-manual) · Also on Amazon (ISBN: 9781625951717)

The official ARRL study manual. More comprehensive than KB6NU but also denser. Updated for the 2023–2027 pool.

**How to use it:**
- Read the chapter corresponding to each week's subelement.
- Best used as the primary reading if you want deeper understanding of concepts beyond just passing the exam.
- Pairs with ARRL Exam Review (free online) — after each chapter, take a chapter-specific quiz.
- If budget is a concern: the KB6NU guide covers the same ground for free. The ARRL manual adds depth, not necessity.

---

### 4. ARRL Exam Practice (free)
**URL:** [arrl.org/exam-practice](https://www.arrl.org/exam-practice)

Free, no account needed. Generates randomized 35-question practice exams from the actual FCC question pool.

**How to use it:**
- Take one full practice exam at the end of each week (starting Week 2).
- Record your score weekly to track progress — you should see steady improvement.
- When you consistently score 30+/35 on three consecutive tests, you're ready.
- No feedback on wrong answers (just correct/incorrect) — use HamStudy for learning, ARRL Exam Practice for testing.

---

### 5. HamExam.org (free)
**URL:** [hamexam.org/view_pool/19-General](https://hamexam.org/view_pool/19-General)

**How to use it:**
- Browse the full question pool organized by subelement. Good for reviewing *all* questions in a section before a practice test.
- Topic-specific quizzes: pick a subelement and drill just those questions.
- No account needed. Less polished than HamStudy but useful for cross-referencing.

---

### 6. Ham Radio School — General Quizzes (free)
**URL:** [hamradioschool.com/general-quizzes-2027.html](https://www.hamradioschool.com/general-quizzes-2027.html)

Subelement-by-subelement quizzes with explanations. Good as a third option if HamStudy or ARRL Exam Practice results feel inconsistent.

---

### 7. Dave Casler Video Series (free on YouTube)
**Search:** "Dave Casler KE0OG General Class" on YouTube

Dave Casler (KE0OG) teaches the full ARRL General curriculum as a structured video course. Videos are 15–30 minutes each, organized by subelement.

**How to use it:**
- Watch the corresponding videos for each week's subelement as supplemental material.
- Especially useful for visual/auditory learners and for complex topics like propagation (G3) and antennas (G9) where a diagram helps.
- Not required if reading + HamStudy is working — add it when a topic isn't clicking from text alone.

---

### 8. OpenExamPrep (free)
**URL:** [open-exam-prep.com/practice/ham-radio-general](https://open-exam-prep.com/practice/ham-radio-general)

100+ practice questions, no signup required. Good for quick drills on mobile or when you want a no-friction practice session.

## Recommended study sequence

### Phase 1 — Setup (30 min, once)
1. Create account at HamStudy.org
2. Download KB6NU No-Nonsense Guide PDF
3. Open the [[05-Resources/Courses/HAM-Radio/General License Syllabus|10-Week Syllabus]] and set your target exam date
4. Block 30–45 min/day in your calendar for the next 10 weeks

### Phase 2 — Weekly study loop (repeat for each subelement)
1. **Read** — KB6NU guide section for the week's subelement (~20 min)
2. **Drill** — HamStudy.org flashcard mode for that subelement until >80% accuracy (~20 min)
3. **Watch** (optional) — Dave Casler video for any topic that didn't click from reading
4. **Test** — End of week: one full 35-question ARRL Exam Practice test. Record score.

### Phase 3 — Final review (Week 10)
1. HamStudy test mode: 3 complete exams in a row, targeting 30+/35 each
2. Drill any subelement below 85% on HamStudy
3. If consistently scoring 30+/35 → schedule the exam

## Connection to vault and other goals

The HAM license study overlaps significantly with other things already in this vault:

**CAP certification overlap** — G0 (Electrical & RF Safety) and G5 (Electrical Principles) cover Ohm's Law, DC circuits, power calculations, and safety thresholds. These same topics appear on the CAP exam under Domain 1 (Measurement) and Domain 3 (Control). Study these subelements with [[07-Work-Knowledge/Automation/Automation|Work Knowledge / Automation]] notes open — you're studying for two credentials at once.

**Work Knowledge** — G8 (Signals & Emissions) and G3 (Propagation) have no work overlap, but G5 circuits and G9 transmission line theory will sharpen your understanding of industrial communication systems.

**Future Vision** — HAM radio is explicitly named in the [[01-Vision/future-vision|3-year vision]] as a knowledge and hobby goal. Completing the General license is a concrete milestone toward that vision.

## After you pass

1. Your callsign appears in the FCC ULS database within 1–2 business days of passing
2. Check it at [arrl.org/obtain-your-amateur-radio-license](https://www.arrl.org/obtain-your-amateur-radio-license)
3. Get on the air — connect to a local repeater first (easiest), then HF once you have equipment
4. Consider POTA (Parks on the Air) — [[05-Resources/Courses/HAM-Radio/Get Started with POTA (Parks On The Air) – Beginners Guide!|already in vault]]
5. Next milestone: **Extra class license** — unlocks all remaining HF frequencies and sub-bands
