# Energy Menus — My Lists

> Pre-decided tactics for the three states where work breaks down: **can't start**, **lost focus**, **running on empty**. Pick one and execute — no deliberating.

---

## 🔥 Activation Menu
*Use when: pre-task dread, post-meeting fog, decision paralysis, bad sleep start*

- [ ] Put on a focus playlist (instrumental / lo-fi)
- [ ] Do 5 slow diaphragm breaths
- [ ] Clear one small admin task (email reply, Todoist triage, file a note)
- [ ] Make coffee or water — set a 5-minute timer, then open the work
- [ ] Write the first sentence of whatever you're avoiding (just one sentence)
- [ ] Open Studio 5000 / the tool, don't commit to anything — just look

---

## 🔄 Reactivation Menu
*Use when: caught daydreaming, tab-hopping, 20 minutes passed and nothing happened*

- [ ] Stand up, 10 jumping jacks or shoulder rolls
- [ ] Put phone in another room
- [ ] Swap playlist to something with a harder beat
- [ ] State out loud: "I am working on [X] until [time]"
- [ ] Close all tabs except the one you need
- [ ] Set a 25-minute Pomodoro — commit to just finishing the timer

---

## 🔋 Recharge Menu
*Use when: brain feels flat, been heads-down for 2+ hours, making dumb mistakes*

- [ ] Walk around the block (no phone)
- [ ] Make coffee or tea — sit away from the desk
- [ ] 5-minute stretching: neck, shoulders, hips
- [ ] Step outside for direct light — even 3 minutes resets alertness
- [ ] Log in vitals note if end of day: `energy:`, `focus_intention:`
- [ ] Close the laptop lid and do nothing for 10 minutes

---

## Usage Notes

- **Don't pick more than one item per episode.** The point is fast execution, not optimization.
- **Activation → Reactivation → Recharge** is roughly the sequence across a workday.
- These live here as a reference — link from Daily Note or Todoist if needed.
