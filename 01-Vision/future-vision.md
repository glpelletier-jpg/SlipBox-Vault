# Future Vision

> Write as if it's already happened. Be specific and sensory.
> Read before each quarterly quest-planning session.

---

## 1-year vision

*What does your life look like 12 months from now?*

**Work:**
It's December 2026 and I've found my footing in my new role. I know what good work looks like here, I've built trust with the people around me, and I'm delivering things I'm proud of. I'm no longer in "learning the ropes" mode — I'm contributing in a way that feels deliberate and growing.

**Health:**
I've completed the Health Reset. I'm exercising at least 4 times a week as a baseline — not as a challenge anymore, just as how I live. I'm sleeping consistently and waking up with actual energy. The physical sluggishness that was dragging everything else down is gone.

**Relationships:**
I'm present with my kids in a way I wasn't before — not just physically there but actually engaged. The mental noise of undone things has quieted enough that I can give them my full attention when we're together.

**Personal:**
I'm creating things I'm proud of again. Something I've shipped — a project, a body of work, something — exists that didn't exist a year ago. I'm curious and building, not just consuming.

## 3-year vision

*What do you want to have built, become, or experienced?*

It's 2029. My career has a clear trajectory — I've grown deliberately, not just by accident of time passing. I'm known for doing great work and being someone people trust. The job I have now has either grown into something more or launched me into something better.

My health is no longer something I have to think about maintaining — the habits from the Health Reset became who I am. My energy is consistent. I show up well for my family and my work because the foundation is solid.

I've created something meaningful outside of work — a project, a body of writing, a skill developed to a level I'm proud of. It connects to my value of creativity and exists in the world beyond just me.

My kids are a few years older and I've been present for it. Not perfect, but genuinely there.

3-year vision much more concrete.
	- Knowledge and wisdom of the things i am interested in:
		- Electronics as a whole and specifically with HAM radios and there use
		- Computer programing both as a hobby and professionally as an Automation Engineer

## 10-year vision

*What is the bigger arc you're living into?*

It's 2036. I've built a career I'm proud of — not just in title or income, but in the quality of the work and the integrity I brought to it. I've been honest and straight with people along the way. I've kept doing what I said I'd do.

My health has been a foundation for everything for a decade. I have the energy to show up for the people I love and the work I care about. I haven't sacrificed my body for short-term output.

My kids are older. I was present for their childhood in a way that mattered — engaged, not distracted, not depleted. That's the thing I'll look back on most.

I've made things I'm proud of. Created output that has impact beyond just me. Stayed curious. Kept building.
