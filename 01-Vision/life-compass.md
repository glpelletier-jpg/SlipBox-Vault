## Core values

*3–5 values with personal definitions. Not dictionary definitions — what does each value mean to you, and what does violating it feel like?*

*The values below are drawn from your Future Vision. Edit them freely — keep, rename, add, remove.*

---

### Presence

**What it means to me:**
> Being actually here — not just physically present but genuinely engaged. Full attention without the background noise of undone things. With my kids: engaged, not distracted. At work: contributing deliberately, not just occupying time.

**What violating it looks like:**
> Being in a room but not in the conversation. Half-listening. Doing the thing while thinking about the next thing.

**How I want to show up:**
> Curious and strong minded

---

### Integrity

**What it means to me:**
> Doing what I said I'd do. Being honest and straight with people — not managing impressions, not softening things to the point of misleading. The career I want is built on doing great work and being someone people trust.

**What violating it looks like:**
> Saying yes when I mean maybe. Letting something slide because calling it out is uncomfortable. Performing progress instead of making it.

**How I want to show up:**
>Comfortable that I do not have to remember any lies, people can depend on what I tell them

---

### Creation

**What it means to me:**
> Making things I'm proud of. Being curious and building, not just consuming. Something exists in the world because I put it there — a body of work, a skill, output that has impact beyond just me.

**What violating it looks like:**
> Months passing in "learning mode" that never becomes making mode. Consuming ideas without producing anything from them. Staying comfortable instead of shipping something real.

**How I want to show up:**
>Doing something

---

### Foundation

**What it means to me:**
> Health as the substrate that everything else runs on. Energy is not separate from work, parenting, or creativity — it's what all three are built on. The physical foundation has to be maintained or everything else degrades.

**What violating it looks like:**
> Treating sleep, exercise, and food as optional or negotiable. Borrowing energy from the future to hit short-term output. The sluggishness that drags everything else down.

**How I want to show up:**
>able to stand straight and enjoy the day fully

---

### Deliberateness

**What it means to me:**
> Growing intentionally, not just by accident of time passing. Knowing what good work looks like and aiming at it. Making decisions about my life rather than just responding to what comes at me.

**What violating it looks like:**
> Looking up after a year and finding I drifted rather than moved. Being busy without a direction. Taking the next logical step because it was there, not because I chose it.

**How I want to show up:**
>better educated with a rounded knowledge set
